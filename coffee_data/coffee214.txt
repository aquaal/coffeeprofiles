<div class="column-right">
<p>This lot comes from the community of Bojonalito in the municipality of La Libertad, Huehuetenango. Altitude in this area spans a range of roughly 1500 meters in town, on up to about 1800 meters in the surrounding hillsides. Like most of the coffee communities in the area, farmers of Bojonalito plant Bourbon and Caturra types for the most part, with the occasional Typica mixed in. Coffee processing is handled at the farmer's home or the home of a neighbor, and then the coffee is patio dried. The dry parchment is transported down to Huehue town where it is dry milled and prepared for final transport to the US. We spent many hours at the cupping table sorting through small producer lots from the region in order to put these 13 bags together. This is part of our "Proyecto Xinabajul" in Huehuetenango, which you can read an in-depth and detailed description of the <a href="https://www.sweetmarias.com/library/content/guatemala-proyecto-xinabajul">HERE</a>.</p> </div>

<p>The dry fragrance smells bittersweet at City+, smells of cocoa and roasted nut, along with understated brown sugar and dried fruit hints are notable. The wet aroma is much more intense and shows perfumed honey-sweet smells, and a candied almond note (like Jordan almonds) in the steam. A demurara-like, raw sugar sweetness underscores the brewed coffee and City+ roasts pull out subtle top notes of dried apple, a subtle black tea flavor and of course a thick layer of unrefined sugary sweetness. At Full City expect darker fruited accents like date pieces, though bittersweet chocolate is the dominant characteristic. Bojonalito is an all around sweet coffee that's pretty forgiving in the roaster. Unless you take it far into 2nd snaps, you can expect a coffee that's balanced, bodied, and reveals mild top notes after cooling off a bit.</p> </div>
