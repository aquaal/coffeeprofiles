<div class="column-right">
<p>Not far from the shores of Lake Kivu lies Karambi, a washing station named after Karambi town where it resides. The altitude at Karambi station is 1700 meters above sea level and the coffee is grown upwards of 2000 meters in the surrounding mountainside. The station was erected to buy coffee from smallholder farmers in the region, many whom also work at the site processing and preparing the coffee for export. Quality grading starts immediately upon delivery, the underripe coffee separated out by floating in tanks of water. They produce wet-process ("fully washed"), honey process and dry process ("naturals") coffee at Karambi and all three undergo intensive sorting technics in order to make sure the A1 grade coffee is made up of the densest seeds. This is a honey process lot and in terms of flavor, fits somewhere between the clean, sweet cup profile of a wet process coffee and more fruit-forward characteristics of a natural. It produces a unique cup profile for Rwanda with a sweetness that holds up to darker roasting.</p> </div>

<p>Karambi honey has a berry sweetness that brings to mind some of our cleaner Ethiopia naturals and hits a floral note in the aroma. The dry fragrance spins a web of fruit and spice smells, dark berry and clove come to mind and pouring the hot water brings up a sweet scent of blackberry sundae topping. The cup is syrupy sweet and fruited flavor notes are complex, with traces of red raspberry, green melon and fresh date showing in light roast brews. The sweetness is certainly fruited but also has flavors of rustic sweeteners like date sugar and blackstrap molasses. The finish has a wheatberry note as well as a tannic mouthfeel like iced black tea. I loved my City+ roast as pour over and will venture to say that Karambi will make a delicious darker roasted coffee as well. In my experience, fruited flavors shift when a fruit-forward coffee is roasted to Full City. It isn't until you reach Full City+ where that side of the cup is compromised. We brought in dry processed and fully washed lots from Karambi as well and expect to have them available by early Summer.</p> </div>
