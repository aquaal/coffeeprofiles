<p>Nutty sweetness and bittersweet chocolate flavors are nicely developed in the somewhat tight range of City+/Full City. The brewed coffee has hefty body and rustic sweet flavors that give way to murky hints of fruit and creamy nut tones that reminded me of Brazil nut and hazelnut. Roast tones in the cup hit the tastebuds like a semi-sweet chocolate bar and leave behind something closer to earth-toned carob in aroma. City+ roasts cool to flavors of sweet peanut sauce and an interesting rustic melon note. Brazils are often softer, lower-density beans. Because of this, they are more prone to roast defects like scorching and tipping which can bring out intense bittering. If your roaster allows, try a lower drop temperature and make less aggressive heat adjustments in order to avoid this. Brazils also seem to benefit from longer resting period post-roast, our recommendation being 48 hours minimum.</p> </div>
