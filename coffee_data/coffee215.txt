<div class="column-right">
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Chalum is one of the highest peaks in La Libertad region of Huehuetenango. Cedros Palmira is on one side of the peak and the road leading to several of the areas we buy from on the other -El Injerto, Paraiso, Bojonalito, etc. This lot is a blend of a coffees from small producers in these neighboring towns, 7 lots to be exact, and a mixture of cultivars that includes Caturra, Catuai, Bourbon and Typica. The altitude ranges from around 1500 meters to 1900 meters and most of the farms are less than 5 acres in size. "Xinabajul" is the name of the project/partnership we have with local coffee people to offer premium prices to farmers that meet quality expectations in the cup. Given that the localities where we are buying coffee are little more than extended family groups, we have found if we involve a brother or sister, they will tell all their coffee-farming kin and soon we have a network of farmers interested in the project. We wrote an in-depth and <a href="https://legacy.sweetmarias.com/library/guatemala-proyecto-xinabajul" rel="noopener" target="_blank">detailed description</a> of the project as well.</span></p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>As odd as it may sound, there's a smell in the dry grounds that I read as "fresh". The coffee<em> is</em> fresh, having just arrived, but it's the mixture of raw sugar, clove spice and a sort of fresh, green herbal note that together have a refrehing quality! The cup is so nice at City+, making a rock-solid Guatemalan brew. I pick up on notes of candied walnuts, milk chocolate and a dried apple note that also helps to underline a malic-type, acidic impression. Chocolate flavors are more 'hints' at City+, but open up to something much more substantial at Full City, with the potential to be quite intense at Full City+ (perhaps next roast test). Espresso shots at Full City have a chocolate-hazelnut flavor and long-lasting bittersweetness.</p> </div>
