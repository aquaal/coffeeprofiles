<div class="column-right">
<p>Calistro Cordoba Garcia was a child when his family moved to the town of La Huaca over 40 years ago. His parents planted coffee at that time and coffee still grows on this land today. Calistro inherited 3 hectares, 2 of which he's passed along to his son, keeping one under his own care. The farm used to be planted entirely in Typica, but in response to a leaf rust ("La Roya") outbreak, Calistro replanted much of the farm in Castillo due its disease resistance and high yields. The Cordoba Garcia clan still pick and process their own coffee as a family, helping each other out across the 3 hectares of shared land, sharing wet-mill equipment and patio space for drying. They float the coffee cherry in water before processing in order to remove as much of the under and over ripe coffee as possible. The coffee is fully-washed and undergoes 24 hour fermentation before being laid to dry on a plastic-lined patio for up to 2 full weeks. The group who coordinated this buying project in Huabal are working to identify farmers who consistently produce good quality coffee, offering cash premiums based on quality tiers. They register all of the farmers they buy from and track payments from the initial base payout to the 2nd payments that are delivered after the coffee finds a home.</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>I've really enjoyed pour-over brews of my City+ roast of this coffee from Calistro Cordova over the last couple of days. Sweet-to-bittering core flavors slot it in with some of our more typical "crowd pleasing" Latin American coffees, but with a little something extra in terms of fruited hints that come up as it cools, but without overpowering the cup. The aroma has something like brown sugar on toast with subtle spice notes such as cinnamon and clove. The cup flavors are tethered to a brown sugar sweetness that fades to some coffee roast bittering in the finish. Balanced sweetness remains at the core of City+ brews (Full City too, but expect more intense bittering) and leaves room for fruited accent notes to crop up in aroma, such as pomegranate and even a cantaloupe note. Another notable characteristic of this lot is acidity, a glimmer of lemon vibrance, which for Peru is exceptional.</p> </div>
