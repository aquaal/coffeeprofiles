<div class="column-right">
<p>Finca El Cipres is a 90 hectare coffee estate located at the Picacho Volcano in the Bálsamo-Quezaltepec coffee region, Nejapa municipality. The Cipres coffee estate has been a part of this community for almost 150 years, most recently coming under the ownership of the Alvarez Gallardo family. The property is nearly 100 hectares in total, 5 of which remain natural forest, the rest devoted to coffee. The main variety in the farm is Bourbon, with smaller plots planted in orange bourbon in the higher areas and sarchimors (hybrids of Villa Sarchi and Timor hybrid) in the lower altitudes. The lowest point in the farm is 1100 meters above sea level, but El Cipres estate reaches all the way up to 1800 meters. El Cipres has been an integral part of the community at large, employing many people from the nearby town, as well as supporting local school and health care initiates. </p> </div>

<p>The ground coffee has smells of graham cracker with a dusting of baking cocoa powder. Adding hot water bolsters the sweet smells and brings about a scent of warm caramel sauce with roasted peanut. The cup packs a dense mouthfeel with pleasant weight that underscores layered chocolate flavors at City+ and Full City (particularly the latter). The middle goes from Hersheys syrup, to semi-sweet chocolate to a bittering baking cocoa flavor in the long finish. There's a hint of carob too - a more earth-toned chocolate substitute. The coffee has mild to moderate apple-like acidity too, and expect roasted nut accent notes. Sure to make a crowd-pleasing French Press that stands up to cream (if you prefer), and also doubles nicely as espresso.</p> </div>
