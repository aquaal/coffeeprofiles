<div class="column-right">
<p>This Guatemala coffee comes from small producers in the Cruz Grande community of Santa Barbara, Huehuetenango, an area with high altitude farms starting at 1500 meters and reaching 2000 in some areas. The farms where this coffee was sourced sit right around 1700 meters and are mostly planted in Caturra, Bourbon and Typica types. This is an area of small holders, not estates like you'd see in the lowlands. The topography wouldn't allow for sprawling farms as the hillside is jutting, and where instead small hamlets are situated in the lands carved out in the surrounding hillside that is sprinkled with countless tiny farms at a couple hectares each. Pulping and fermentation of the coffee is handled locally, and the coffee is, for the most part, patio-dried. Dry-milling happens at a mill in Huehuetenango town where they are equipped with state of the art equipment, and with the infrastructure to properly handle small lots of coffee. "Xinabajul" is the name of the project/partnership we have with local coffee people to offer higher prices to farmers that meet our quality expectations in the cup. Given that the localities where we are buying coffee are little more than extended family groups, we have found if we involve a brother or sister, they will tell all their coffee-farming kin and soon we have a network of farmers interested in our project. We wrote an in-depth and <a href="https://www.sweetmarias.com/library/content/guatemala-proyecto-xinabajul" rel="noopener" target="_blank">detailed description</a> of the project as well.</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>What a lovely coffee this lot from Cruz Grande makes. City and City+ roasts are on the fruitier side for Guatemalan coffee, though still has that balanced core sweetness that we expect in all our Guatemalan coffees. Cup flavors can present so differently when tasted from a spoon, which is still somewhat the case here. But I'm surprised by how much of the fruited sweetness comes through when brewed as a pour-over (I'm drinking a cup while I write!). The aroma has elements of fruit pulp and grape, dark sugary sweetness accented by a hint of maraschino cherry. The cup flavors have a grape note that lends a juiciness to the profile, along with orange and cherry to lesser extents. City+ is all it takes to develop bittersweetness to something much more prominent in the cup, a deep chocolate/cacao flavor working its way into the flavor matrix without obfuscating the rest. Full City roasts further develop core bittersweetness and brings out a dark berry flavor as well.</p> </div>
