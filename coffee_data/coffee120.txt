<div class="column-right">
<p>Thageini Factory is part of Aghuti Farmers Cooperative Society (FCS), an FCS that includes a few other stations we buy from: Gititu and Kagumo. It's not the "factory" as we might imagine it. "Factories" are essentially small washing stations aligned with a particular "society" in Kenya, what we would call a "cooperative". We return to the societies who seem to regularly produce some of the best Kenya coffees, and each year we come across societies that are new to us as well: such as Aghuti. This coffee was purchased direct, not through the Kenya auction system, so we could avoid the risk of losing it. To do this we pay a price that is higher than what the top auction bid might be, but it means we get the exact lot we want. During the final dry milling, the coffee seeds are separated by size which is measured in 1/64ths of an inch, and they call these separations "out turns". The main ones we're used to seeing in specialty coffee are AA 17-19 screen, AB 15-17 screen and Peaberry 15 screen, which this lot is. </p> </div>

<p>The smells in the ground coffee have a sweetness of palm sugar, along with dark berry and citrus peel hints. The wetted crust blooms to a caramel-sweet aroma with wafts of dried fruits in the steam. Dark sugar sweetness is marked by orange zest accent notes, a tangy note that cuts right through the bittersweet layer. City+ roast level is where this coffee really opens up. The brew produces an undercurrent of caramelized sugar flavors that come off like dark caramel, torched sugar and toffee. The cooling cup reveals deeper fruited notes like dried currant and cranberry, along with a cinnamon spiced accent note that is most noticeable in the finish. For Kenya, Thageini Peaberry isn't the brightest of the bunch but does show flashes of rindy citrus, even in darker, Full City roasts. This is a Kenya I want to drink as espresso. A chocolate-y, fruited shot of espresso that will pair nicely with a ribbon of orange zest on the cup rim.</p> </div>
