<div class="column-right">
<p>Since buying our first honey process Sumatra last year, we've come across a few more folks putting in the extra work required to produce what is an atypical process method for the region. Unlike the typical wet hulled process method (giling basah) where coffee is peeled to the seed immediately after depulping and still wet, honey process involves depulping the coffee and drying with the fruit intact. One of the most important distinctions between these two is that the parchment is left intact during honey processing (wet and dry processing too, for that matter), a much needed layer of protection for the drying coffee. This means none of the herbal, earthy notes you probably expect from Sumatra, but rather much more refined, clean cup flavors. This particular honey process lot is the product of a coffee collector named Aulia who buys in the districts of Bebesen and Kute Panang. Farms in these regions tend to span an altitude range of about 1300 to 1600 meters and are planted in older, long berry types (you'll see the long, canoe shaped beans) Abysinnia, Ateng and Tim Tim. In the scheme of things, Aulia's operation is quite small. He usually buys a few bags at a time, and then processes coffee with family at home. This year we paid a small premium for the coffee in order to help fund a drying room so that he can dry coffee inside on raised drying beds. This is 10 bag honey lot will be followed by a larger, wet hulled coffee later in April or early May. Be on the lookout.</p>

<p>You've tried Sumatra before and didn't like it. I get it. While I'm personally a fan, I understand why the earthy sweetness and odd herbal notes that come from wet hulling aren't for everyone. But this honey-processed Sumatra is SO different, clean and sweet, and it shows from the get-go in the cup. The dry fragrance at City roast level has a sweet smell of clover honey and a mild berry note. The wet aromatics have a pleasant marshmallow-like smell, along with dried fruit accents. The underlying sweetness in the cup has characteristics of raw honey and dried fruits, flavor notes parsed out as raisin and tamarind, with traces of bittering black tea and burned sugar in the finish. The cup is overall clean, especially next to one of our better Grade 1 wet-hulled coffees (which cupped together, though a bit like comparing apples to oranges). Full City roasts activate dark cocoa roast flavors that are offset by a fruited sweetness, albeit milder than lighter roasts.</p> </div>
