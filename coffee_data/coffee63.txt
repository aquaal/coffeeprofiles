<div class="column-right">
<p>Qonqana is one of a handful of coffee stations run by the Daye Bensa group in Sidama's mountainous Bensa highland growing region. Unlike their other coffee processing sites (Shantawene, Mirado, Bombe, etc), Qonqana station is outfitted with a Penagos water conserving depulper, which strips away most of the fruit from the seed using very little water. This means they can forgo the fermentation stage to remove the final mucilage layer, instead, soaking overnight in clean water and agitating away what little fruit is left the following morning. Asefa Dukamo is one of the leaders of Daye Bensa group. Qonqana rests above the Qonqana river at 2000+ meters above sea level, which along with slowing coffee maturation and increasing bean density, the coffees beans from this high altitude region can also be quite small in size. The majority of the coffee is 15+, but we are spotting quite a few 13 and 14 screen beans in there too. The biggest concern here is that these smaller, lower density beans will roast faster than the largest coffee. While that can be true, we found that either sticking to City to City+ roast range or slowing down the last leg of the roast helps keep the small beans from charring and darker 'roast' flavors at bay. I don't recommend roasting to Full City or beyond for this reason unless you are shooting for espresso. If you're roasting on a Behmor, it's worth sticking to 150g or less and giving the grid drum a good shake to let the tiniest fall through the mesh rather than in the bottom of the roast chamber.</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>This showstopper Sidama coffee boasts beautiful florals, complex tea and fruit notes, and a transparent, sweet backdrop. The dry fragrance is so floral and perfrumed, with the potency of a standout Gesha lot. Berry fruit and jelly notes infuse the steam of the wet aroma, along with a crystalized sweetness of raw sugar, and perfumed with sweet flowery highlights. City and City+ roasts are downright delicious and produce all the tea-like and floral complexity I could hope for in a Sidama coffee. Right out of the gate, the floral highlight notes are front and center and take the form of star jasmine flowers in full bloom and pearl jasmine green tea. The cup cools to show a fruited side that hints at notes of muscat grape, lychee jelly and fruit gum. Roast this coffee light and you will be rewarded. My brightest, most complex and floral brews were from roasts that I kept very light and roasted on the faster side. In a Quest M3s, this translated to 100g of coffee hitting 1st crack right around 6 minutes in, then pulling the roast just 1 minute and 30 seconds later. I don't think you can roast quite that fast on a Behmor, but from my experience, I'd recommend starting with 150g at the highest manual heat setting, hitting cool 45 seconds to a minute into 1st C and adjusting from there. We only have a limited amount of this coffee and will not be offering 20 lb bags. </p> </div>
