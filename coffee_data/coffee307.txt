<div class="column-right">
<p>Sitio Cocho Velho is a single farm in the Lambari region of Minas Gerais. The farm has been in the same family for many years, currently under the care of the de Fatima family. They inherited Cocho Velho from their grandparents back in 1994 and since then have expanded coffee production to 15 hectares, mostly planted in Yellow and Red Catuaí. Regina de Fatima is a member of an all-female farmer group in Lambari Municipality, the Association of Coffee Women Entrepeneurs, or  "AMECAFE" for short with more than 150 members. Under their management, the de Fatima's have entered Sitio Cocho Velho in local competitions, placing top-10 in Best of Carmo and AMECAFE Women in Coffee competitions.  </p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>Sitio Cocho Velho is a mild Brazil cup with soft nutty sweetness and mild spiced flecks in the aroma. The fragrance and wet aroma have buttery toffee sweetness that hints at nut brittle candy. The cup shows best when roasted to a minimum of City+. You really need that extra roast development in order to pull out sugar caramelization. Cup flavors have aspects of nut brittle as well, a mild caramel candy note with roasted peanut and sesame seed accents. Cocoa is fairly absent at City+, but casts a darker shadow at Full City. A nice, neutral cup on its own that is sure to work well as a Brazil blend ingredient in espresso blending too.</p> </div>
