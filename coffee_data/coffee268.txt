<div class="column-right">
<div>This lot from the Panawa region of Cipray comes from a small coffee company that are relatively new in Garut. They actually got their start as a minivan dealer in Bandung and through connections forged with coffee collectors buying their vehicles, they decided to invest in the coffee sector! Central to the operation is a wet mill and coffee-drying warehouse in Garut where they process all of their coffee. This lot was purchased from a coffee collector in Panawa village named Pak Nyang (seen in the photo with the cowboy hat). He buys coffee from a little more than 100 farmers in Panawa whose farms range in altitude from 1200 to 1500 meters above sea level. You see a lot of old Typica in these parts, though more and more Ateng is being planted and coffee is intercropped with pine trees in an agroforestry area. This is a wet-hulled coffee, not the typical washed that we often buy from Java. This type of processing groups it with Sumatra in terms of flavor, body and acidity, an earth-toned coffee that has robust bittersweetness too. The defect count tends to be higher with this style of processing, so you can expect drastic color variance in the green coffee, some bug holes and a few quakers in the roasted coffee. Even so, it is a much cleaner sort than most of our Grade 1 Sumatran coffees.</div> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>At Full City, the dry fragrance has a bittersweet smell that's rustic-tinged, like an earth-toned, "bean to bar" chocolates. Foresty notes proliferate in fragrance and aroma as does an underlying syrupy, yet rustic, sweetness. The flavors in the cup are akin to a wet hulled Sumatra, the defining cup characteristics much the same. You'll find a big-bodied, low-toned cup, rustic sweetness that is somewhere in between palm and date sugar. Accent notes are numerous, complex and hints at sweet tobacco, roasted corn and something like forest ground cover. Full City+ roasts manage to develop such intense bittering that most of the earth tones almost fall away completely. I really enjoyed my Full City+ roast and highly recommend this coffee for a dark-roasted, milk drink option or espresso (will work well as a bittersweet, bodied blend base too).</p> </div>
