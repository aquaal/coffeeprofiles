<div class="column-right">
<p>This coffee from Donaldo Villatoro, one member of the Villatoro family, a group we buy coffee from in the Huehuetenango highlands. Donaldo's farm is in Cuevas, aptly named for the small caves tucked away in the rocky outcrops of the mountainside. Coffee is grown at an altitude of roughly 1700 to 1800 meters above sea level, and typical varietals are Bourbon and Caturra, with Typica sprinkled in. The Donaldo shares a wet mill with a few family members. They pull water from springs above, but are working to capture clean water from the river that runs below the farm and is punctuated by a 50 foot waterfall a bout 100 meters from the farm! This coffee comes to us through a joint-effort of a local micro-mill owner who is able to process and separate the micro-lots we approve, and a highly sophisticated dry-mill who handle the finishing touches and export the coffees for us. It's also part of our "Proyecto Xinabajul" small holder project in the region, which you can read more about <a href="https://legacy.sweetmarias.com/library/guatemala-proyecto-xinabajul/">HERE</a>.</p> </div>

<p>The dry fragrance is touched by warming spice and sweet, natural sugars at City roast level, with bittering roast smells cropping up at Full City. The steam smells infused with the sweetness of warm oatmeal cookies, brown sugar, raisin and honey potency. The cup has a brisk acidic impression at City and City+ roast level, that plays off flavor notes of the toasted sugar sweetness nicely. It's the interplay of sweetness, spice and structuring acidity that drew us to Donaldo's coffee in the first place. The cup cools to accent notes of Earl Grey, dried plum, and cardamom spice in the finishing aroma. It's delicious! Full City roasts are also very nice, clean cup flavors throughout, and manages to pull out a juicy, dark fruit note.</p> </div>
