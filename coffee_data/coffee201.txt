<div class="column-right">
<p>The farm Finca Los Suspiros is a joint effort between Axel Palacios and Fredy Morales in the town of San Gaspar Ixchil. You may recognize Fredy's name from his family-farm in San Pedro Necta, Finca Rosma. He is also the central person in Huehuetenango who has helped to make Proyecto Xinabajul a success by spreading the word about our project and managing all of our buying in the region. "Los Suspiros" means "the sighs". I like to imagine a breath of fresh air, which you get a lot of when taking in the endless vistas of massive valleys when visiting the area. Los Suspiros is mostly planted in Caturra and Bourbon and they are producing washed, honey and natural process coffees. This is a full honey-process lot, perhaps our favorite of the samples we tried from the farm during our February visit. Altitude at Los Suspiros starts is right around 1800 meters above sea level.</p> </div>

<p>True to the process-type name, the dry fragrance kicks off with impressive honey-sweet smells, light and clean, along with buttery florals and a golden raisin hint. The wet grounds have an aroma of 'baked goods', like peach scone or bran muffin, with dried fruit. The brew is so tasty at City+ roast with stone fruit and roasted sunflower seed accents atop creamy honey sweetness underneath. For honey process, Los Suspiros shows convincing level of acidity that impresses and imparts apple-like vibrance on the cup profile. The cooling cup produces fruit pastry flavors (similar to aroma) like an apricot danish with the creamy fruit center and honey granola with raisin. We only roasted to City and City+, but I'd like to try this coffee at Full City too as I'm certain the honeyed sweetness will hold up. Our lightest City roast didn't quite hit the mark in terms of sweetness and as such is outside our recommended roast range.</p>
