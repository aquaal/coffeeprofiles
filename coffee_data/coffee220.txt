<div class="column-right">
<p>The coffees that make up this lot are from the La Libertad Municipality in Huehuetenango. The farms making up this lot stretch up into the hillside villages that top out around 1800 meters. Most farmers are planting a mix of cultivars, Bourbon, Caturra, Typica and Catai are typically what you see from one to the next. Wet processing is handled locally, and then the coffee is patio dried, before being transported down to a dry mill in Huehuetenango town where the parchment layer is removed, the coffee sorted, and prepped for export. We spent many hours at the cupping table sorting through small producer lots from the region in order to put this rather small lot together. There are actually two lots being sold under this record as they cupped identical. One lot is from the village of Bojonalito, the other from neighboring Paraiso.</p> </div>

<p>The aromatic profile is built on toasted sugars, caramelized and smoky sweet as the roast develops, with hints of dark brewed tea. Brown sugar smells found in the dry fragrance carry over into the wet aroma as a sort of butter pecan smell, like sweet cream and toasted nut. The brewed coffee is a real crowd pleaser, something for everyone no matter your roast preference. City and City+ roasts have unrelenting sweetness of unrefined sugars, a little bit of roasted almond in the back end, as well as a tea-like vibrance that adds life to the bittersweet cup. As it cools, subtle fruit accents hint at black currant and lemon grass in the finish. There is a cocoa powder note in the aftertaste that moves up to poll position when roasted to Full City and Full City+. No matter where your roast winds up, La Libertad is a bodied cup with the weight of apple juice, an acidic impression present without being the coffee's focal point. My Full City+ roast made a bittersweet single-origin espresso shot with dominant flavors of high cocoa content chocolate bar, aromatic highlights of crisped rice and dried pomegranate seed.</p> </div>
