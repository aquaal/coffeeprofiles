<p>The cooperative Matyazo lies in the highlands beyond the shores of Lake Kivu in Rwanda's Ngororero district. The coop is made up of small farmers, most of whom have less than a single hectare of coffee planted. The wet mill sits at an altitude of 1700 meters and coffee grows up the steep, mountain slopes, reaching peaks of 2100 meters above sea level. Like much of Rwanda, the coffee planted in the region is Bourbon variety. The wet mill processes many batches of coffee each season that are kept separate in order to identify a range of quality from one process lot to the next. This particular lot that stood out to us is 25 bags in total. It's worth mentioning that Matyazo won the Rwanda's Cup of Excellence coffee competition a few years back.</p> </div>

<p>There's a fruit and spice concoction that is raised from the ground coffee, and has elements of cinnamon and sugar and pineapple upside down cake. After pouring water, the crust that forms at the top of the glass (or in a pour over, drip cone) smells like warm gingerbread with an underlying sweetness reminiscent of molasses syrup, along with raisin and a hint of buckwheat pancakes and maple syrup. The cup has a lot of brown sugar sweetness, unprocessed with molasses. Nutmeg spice shows up in the darker roasts with a more raisiny sweetness at City to City+. Body seems quite substantial and gives this coffee some heft in terms of intensity, especially in the darker roast levels where burnt sugars imbue a pungency to the finish. It has top-end notes with fruit acidity, but this knits well into the dried fruit and sugars in the cup. It's a big, balanced coffee, a very complete cup, ticking boxes for brightness, complexity and overall character.</p> </div>
