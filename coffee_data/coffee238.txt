<div class="column-right">
<p>Sergio and Walter Romero's farm La Lesquinada is located in the Lesquinas area, not far outside Corquin. Altitude varies, and the farm is broken into several plots, this being managed by Sergio, the highest 10 hectare plot reaches just over 1600 meters above sea level. This area is also home to a lot of larger farms, several of which are now abandoned due to rust outbreak some time back. Sergio and his brother Walter saw their own rust attack a few years back prompting them to chop down almost the entire farm and replant in resistant varieties. Can't say I blame them for wanting the insurance provided by planting disease resistant coffee. 32 hectares in total, the cultivars currently under production are a mix of Parainema, and Icatu, along with some smaller plots of Bourbon, Caturra, Typica, Pacamara, and Gesha. The coffee is milled not far from the farm using a mechanical demucilager and both sun dried on patios and inside greenhouses with raised drying beds. The Romero's own the mill as well and run a local coffee association where they process the coffee from neighboring farms and help gain members access to the global coffee market. </p> </div>

<p>The dry fragrance has a sweet smell of dark sugar and black currant, with subtle coriander hints. Pouring hot water brings up a sweet, somewhat herbaceous aroma, and I find the sweetness to have a pungent smokey quality that is as bitter as it is sweet. It's a bittersweet tasting coffee across the roast spectrum, both well-balanced especially in the City+ to Full City roast territory. Middle roasts have a nice brown sugar flavor, with bittering cocoa flavors that are velvety and resonate in the finish. As the cup temperature dips a bit top notes like raisin and candied yam come through, with an aspect of sweet green herbs not far behind, along with soft, citrus rind-like acidity. Our Full City roast kicked up a cloud of inky chocolate roast flavors like ganache and roasted nibs. Compact bittersweetness in both brewed coffee and espresso applications that will surely stand up to milk. </p> </div>
