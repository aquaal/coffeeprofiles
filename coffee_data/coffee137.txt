<div class="column-right">
<p>Tucked away in the Nyamasheke region of Rwanda's Western Province is the Kanyege washing station site. At 1870 meters above sea level, Kanyege rests in the hills just above the Kanzu site, and the coffee they are processing comes from farms as high as 2000 meters. Farmers in this region are growing older Bourbon cultivars, and while Kanyege produces mostly wet process, they also produce a small amount of dry process coffee too (we've carried it in the past). They are floating cherry and sorting by hand in order to remove the majority of underripe coffee, then employing more hand labor to sort out any potential defects after the coffee is washed. The extra care put into green coffee preparation is apparent when you see the coffee green and roasted, with very few inconsistencies physically or in the cup.</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>A delicate touch in the dry fragrance is articulated as cinnamon powder accenting caramelized sugars. City+ roasts have such a sweet aroma, browned sugar notes emanate from the steam, accented by a mild raisin note. The cup is very sweet at City/City+ and presents a brew that is balanced and nuanced as it cools. It shows a touch of honey in sweetness that alludes to wheat puffs when roasted light. Kanyege has slight herbal character that along with fairly vibrant citric acidity comes off like a mixture of lemon grass and black teas. Full City roasts have dark chocolate roast tones, creamy characteristics like whipped chocolate ganache. Kanyege as espresso is a real hit. Full City shots extract tart orange rind note at the lead that quickly moves into flavors of high % cacao bar with a dusting of baking spice in the finish. Kanyege seems to be delicious no matter how you brew it!</p> </div>
