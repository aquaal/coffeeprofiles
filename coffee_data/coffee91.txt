<div class="column-right">
<p>This coffee comes from a privately owned farm and processing site in Shantawene Village, tucked away in the Bombe mountains of Ethiopia's Sidama growing region. Along with coffee from their own estate called "Daye Bensa", they are buying and processing coffee from local farmers from different growing areas nearby.  This lot is a mix of coffees harvested from outgrowers around Shantawene village. Although the wet mill is certified organic, we did not bring this coffee in as such this year. Being certified means that they register every small farmer who they buy from and certify that organic agricultural practices are being employed at the farm level (they also hold Rain Forest Alliance and UTZ certification). The farm and outgrowers are growing Ethiopian heirlooms and altitude ranges from just under 2000 meters to 2100 meters above sea level. It's worth noting that the screen size is on the small side too. The majority of the coffee is 15+, but we are spotting a few 14 screen beans in there too, though nowhere near the amount of small beans as last year. This lot is much more suitable for grid drums such as the Behmor. We don't recommend roasting Shantawene beyond City+ anyways in order to keep the focus on the light, bright and floral flavor profile.</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>This wet process lot from Shantawene has confectionery sweetness in the dry grounds, with light touches of sweet spice, stone fruit and a perfumed floral hint. The wet aroma has honey appeal, along with an overt jasmine quality that we tend to find in very nice wet-processed Yirga Cheffe and Guji coffees. Intense sweetness translates in the cup too. City and City+ roasts offer a somewhat transparent sweetness, like sugar in the raw or simple syrup, that breathes life into the cup flavors (sweetness scored a whopping 9.4!). A vibrant citrus note shines bright in lighter roasts, and with that intense underlying sweetness underneath, has an aspect of honey tangerine. As the coffee cools off you do catch a glimpse of fresh flowers, perfumed accents of pearl jasmine green tea and fresia. Give it some time after brewing and light stone fruit and berry flavors start to surface, as do soft baking spice finishing highlights and a very nice and complex rue, herbal note. This is an incredibly clean Ethiopian coffee with a level of sweetness and complexity that is nothing short of 'competition level' when roasted light to medium.</p>
