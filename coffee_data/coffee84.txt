<div class="column-right">
<p>Hangadhi is the name of the village where this coffee comes from, or "kebele" as it's referred to in Ethiopia. The coffee comes from a processing site operated by Guji Highland Farm, a group who manage their own large semi-forest coffee farms around the Guji area, as well as processing sites where they process their farm coffee as well as coffee from outgrowers in the area. The particular coffee site that this coffee is from processes coffee from several of the small, surrounding kebele's, separating each process batch by village name. So in this case, it is all coffee from the single kebele Hangadhi. Other kebeles Guji Highland are buying from nearby including Dambi Uddo, Hangadhi, Daanisa, Korommi and more. For the natural process lots like this one, the coffee cherry is floated first to remove underripe coffee, then laid to dry on raised beds for 3 full weeks (give or take a few days depending on sun). Guji Highland owner, Wodessa, cooked us an incredible goat dinner when we visited last December and he insisted on putting us up for the night as well. Lucky us, as it was sundown and we were still hours away from our final destination! </p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>Hangadhi is an intense dry process coffee in terms of raw, rustic sweetness and across a range of roasts and delicious chocolate roast tones in the darker roasts. The dry fragrance is full with sweet smells of molasses and dark brown sugar, a brownie batter scent at City+ and Full City and hints of caramelized pineapple. The wet aroma has a woody, chai spice note leading into dried orange peel and with root beer-like sweetness. Both our City and Full City roasts brewed intense cups, the lighter of the two showing Guiness stout-like sweetness, very much a chocolate malted grain flavor that is accented by complex aromatics of brown spices such as nutmeg and star anise.  The cup is far from pristine, which is no surprise given the process method, most obvious in aftertaste that is marked by rustic cacao, brown rice syrup mingling with somewhat murky flavors of stewed fruits and berries. Full City roasting brings these rustic cocoa roast tones to the forefront which come off like dark chocolate bar with high cocoa solids; think 85%+ cacao with a pleasantly bittering affect. The chocolate flavors come through so clearly that I may prefer this coffee dark roasted. That may be a first for Ethiopia! The acidity level is rolled off a lot in these darker roast levels and body is full and opaque, making this a prime espresso component too.</p> </div>
