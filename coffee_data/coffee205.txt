<div class="column-right">
<p>The farm Finca Los Suspiros is a joint effort between Axel Palacios and Fredy Morales in the town of San Gaspar Ixchil. You may recognize Fredy's name from his family-farm in San Pedro Necta, Finca Rosma. He is also the central person in Huehuetenango who has helped to make Proyecto Xinabajul a success by spreading the word about our project and managing all of our buying in the region. "Los Suspiros" means "the sighs". I like to imagine a breath of fresh air, which you get a lot of when taking in the endless vistas of massive valleys when visiting the area. Los Suspiros is mostly planted in Caturra and Bourbon and they are producing washed, honey and natural process coffees. This is a fully-washed lot, perhaps our favorite of the samples we tried from the farm last year. Altitude at Los Suspiros starts at around 1800 meters above sea level.</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>As the name suggests, "Los Suspiros" is like a breath of fresh air. The cup seems to have ample sweetness whether light or dark-roasted, and the dense, high-grown beans proved to be easy to roast and really quite forgiving. The sweetness in the ground coffee at City has smells of cinnamon-raisin bread, malt-like, honey wheat and subtle spice. Adding the hot water really brings up the aromatic compounds and sweet smells of brown sugar and dark dried fruit (think raisins cooked in oatmeal) infuse the steam coming from the crust. The cup at City and City+ shows berry-like brightness that feeds into a fruited accent note of blueberry. But at the core of Los Suspiros is a foundation of raw sugar sweetness like simple brown sugar that is contrasted by subtle accent notes of red apple, baking spice and fading to a finishing flavor of cinnamon-laced chocolate bar. Full City may tip the flavor scale of sweet vs. bitter toward the latter, though still comes off as a balance the two. I may prefer City+ myself, but this coffee's sweetness is sure to hold up into 2nd snaps. </p> </div>
