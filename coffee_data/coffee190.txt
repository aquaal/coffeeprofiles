<div class="column-right">
<p>Finca Melanie is a farm run by Melanie Hererra, a woman who has played a large role in our sourcing of Guatemalan coffees, especially in the Antigua region. She works for a local Antiguan, family-run exporter who also own several estates in the area (Pulcal, Cabrejo should sound familiar). For the most part, Melanie's roll has been to provide agronomical support to the farms as well as act as a sort of liaison for clients like us on behalf of the exporter. A few years back, Melanie bought her own farm in the Hunapu region, not far from Ciudad Vieja on the slopes of Volcan de Agua. This is an area of small-holders (you may have seen our small producer Hunapu blends), and at 2 manzanas in size (roughly 2 hectares), Finca Melanie is no exception. The farm currently has 900 coffee trees, mostly Caturra, Typica and Sarchimor, and sits at 1875 meters above sea level. This first lot from Finca Melanie is just 400 lbs and we have high hopes for a shot at a bit more than that this next harvest. The coffee has a simple, sweet, clean cup profile with exceptional acidity for coffee from this region. Altitude no doubt helps with that since the colder climate facilitates a slower fruit maturation, and denser seed. Because of the limited quantity, Finca Melanie is only available in 1 - 5 LB bags.</p> </div>

<p>This coffee from Finca Melanie has smells of raw sugar and black currant at City roast level, and more roast bittersweetness at Full City/Full City+. It's clean and sweet when brewed, simple to some degree in light roasts, whereas medium roasts develop a nice dark fruit accent note. The wet aroma has a caramelized sweetness with a whiff of cinnamon powder. My lightest roast produced a moderate level of raw sugar sweetness that backed top notes of Sencha tea, and honey comb. Tart acidity permeates the cup when roasted light, rounded off some at Full City, though still present. It's not 'Kenya' bright, but bracing for Guatemala! The cool coffee has a grape-like accent note that offers something on the refreshing side to a warm cup of coffee. Full City brews are chocolatey and the fruited hints plump up to dark berry, accenting balanced bittersweetness as the coffee cools.</p> </div>
