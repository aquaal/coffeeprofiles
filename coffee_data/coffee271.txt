<div class="column-right">
<p>This coffee is from a newly-started mill in a small town Tolajuk within the Luwu District. The town sits in the Eastern slopes of Mount Latimojong, which boasts to highest peak in Sulawesi. The folks who run the mill purchase coffee in whole cherry form via small holders in and around the village. They are only processing two different styles of honey process coffee at the moment, yellow and red. This is the latter, which in this particular case involves submerging the whole cherry in barrels of water for a day in order to start a fermentation process. They then peel the outer layer of the cherry with mechanical demucilage machine, leaving much of the fruit intact for the drying period. The fruit dries to a different hue of yellow or red depending on how much of the fruit is left intact, hence the names (check out the last photo and you can see the difference in color). The coffee is dried on raised beds over the course of roughly 2 weeks, give or take, then finally stored in sealed bags for a resting period for about 30 days. Farms in this area span an altitude range of about 1250 to 1450 meters and are planted in Typica types and hybrids including Jember, S-795 and Lini-S.</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>Light roasts bring out mild fruited characteristics in Tana Luwu, partly a bi-product of the honey processing technique. Unlike wet and dry process coffees, honey is sort of a middle ground where the fruit skin is mechanically removed but some of the fruit remains intact during the entire drying period. This can lead to a more fruited cup and often retaining some of the acidic qualities that might be compromised by full natural processing. But unlike its red honey counterpart we sold earlier in the year, this lot is a lot less-fruited overall and yields more of a bittersweet cup at City+ and beyond. Tana Luwu has a nice, buoyant body and the base sweet flavor is a bit like dark corn syrup. Accent notes have subtle spiced hints, roasted pumpkin seed and a sweet gourd note that reminds me of sweet potato pie filling. Fruits are very subtle and I pick up on suggestions of dried pear. There's an earth-toned aspect underneath that lends to a 'tanned leather' aromatic note too. My Full City roast was delicious, chocolate and body are more of the central cup characteristics and will surely double as a nice espresso too.</p> </div>
