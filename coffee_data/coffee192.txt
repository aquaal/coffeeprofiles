<div class="column-right">
<p>Pulcal is an old coffee estate overlooking the colonial city of Antigua and sits at 1900 meters above sea level. The farm dates to early colonial times (check out the neat tile crest in the first photo below), but coffee was planted in the late 1800's and first exported in 1918. The farm is said to have a unique micro-climate in the Antigua valley, as well as hillside exposure and well-draining soils. The farm is planted in old Typica and Bourbon cultivar trees, mostly red, but also a small amount of yellow fruit, as well as Java trees. This is a separation of the Java cultivar, known for it's elongated, narrow shape (a little like Gesha that way) and often presenting tea-like and spiced top notes as well as moderate acidity.</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>My lightest City roast presented a delicate roasted barley tea note in fragrance, outlined by dark sweetness and mild spice. The wet aroma has a toasted sugar pungency to the smell, a sweetness that is enmeshed with softer roast-tone bittering. The cup is very sweet in the City to Full City roast range, the most developed end of this spectrum being weighed down with cocoa bittersweets. Light roasts start off sweet and balanced. They finish that way too. But as the coffee cools, the top end of the cup livens up some. To be honest, it's not as wild as I'd imagined for Java cultivar. But complex accent notes like black tea with roasted grain and lemon balm herbal hints are found amidst the balanced core coffee flavors. It's an overall sweet, clean cup, with a dynamic acidic impression most pointed in light roasts.</p>
