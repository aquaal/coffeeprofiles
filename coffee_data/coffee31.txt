<div class="column-right">
<p>Rio Combeima stretches down to  the city of Ibagué from the snow-capped volcano of Mount Tolima, that at 17,000+ feet above sea level, is a fixture on the horizon from so many different parts of the country. The two coffee communities that make up the Rio Combeima blend - Machín and China Alta - occupy the east and west sides of this river and are hubs for a new Tolima buying project that we are a part of. The intermediary we work with operate in the area by meeting with local farmers, visiting farms and process sites, and of course, tasting samples. They offer to pay farmers a premium for the opportunity to separate out coffee that meet a particular quality target, coffee that would otherwise be sold off for much lower, base prices and blended away as large Excelso blends by the local associations. Altitude in both coffee towns spans a range of 1850 and 1950 meters above sea level and the cultivars typically grown are Caturra and Variedad Colombia. Some of the farmers are mixing batches from up to a few days in the fermentation tanks, changing the water with each day's addition in order to keep the coffee from over fermenting. This is part of the reason the coffee is fruit forward for a wet-process coffee.</p> </div>

<p>A fragrance of caramel and dry fruit escapes the ground coffee, tamarind and stone fruit were my first thoughts, even though the caramel-like sweetness is more intense. The fruited smells are more pronounced in wet aroma and fold into brown sugar sweetness that come together like the smell of holiday fruit cake. Combeima makes a delicious, sweet cup at City+, raw sugar flavors with molasses hints give way to notes of winey apple and plum. The fruit flavors are a little on the murky side, but no doubt add to cup complexity. Mouthfeel is tannic like black tea and accompanied by a mild cranberry acidic indication, as well as aroma. Cinnamon spice and panela sugar aspects comes up as the coffee cools and make for a contemplative finish. Sweetness will hold up to roast, but my City/City+ roasts were a notch above the cup profile at  a darker, Full City roast level.</p> </div>
