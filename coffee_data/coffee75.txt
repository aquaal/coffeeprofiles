<div class="column-right">
<p>The coffee comes from a processing site called "Korommi" that is operated by Guji Highland Farm, a group who manage their own large semi-forest coffee farms around the Guji area, as well as processing sites where they process their farm coffee as well as coffee from outgrowers in the area. The particular coffee site that this coffee is from processes coffee from several of the small, surrounding kebele's, separating each process batch by village name. So in this case, it is all coffee from the single kebele Korommi. Other kebeles Guji Highland are buying from nearby including Dambi Uddo, Hangadhi, Daanisa, Hangadhi and more. For the natural process lots like this one, the coffee cherry is floated first to remove underripe coffee, then laid to dry on raised beds for 3 full weeks (give or take a few days depending on sun). Guji Highland owner, Wodessa, cooked us an incredible goat dinner when we visited last December and he insisted on putting us up for the night as well. Lucky us, as it was sundown and we were still hours away from our final destination! </p> </div>

<p>Korommi presents a unique cup profile that rides the edge between aromatic fruit and rustic flavor undertones. The dry fragrance smells strong with dark berry, cacao nib and a whiff of new leather. The wet aroma is a heady mix of fruits stewed with raw sugars, contrasted by squash and earth-toned accents. Korommi's cup changes a lot as the coffee cools. That's assumed for most coffee, as heat tends to hinder your ability to taste. But this seemed a bit more extreme to me and also the opposite of what I expected. Just-off brew temp is where Korommi tastes the most vibrant. Strawberry and raspberry flavors come alive and are propped up by bracing, berry-like acidity. Fruited flavors have aspects of both fresh and dried, and even a cooked fruit flavor that reminded me of strawberry-rhubarb pie. Fruits never really fade as the cup cools, but rather, notes of earthy cacao and sandalwood start to grow and you're left with a complex range of flavors from fruit-forward to rustic bittersweets. Korommi loses some of its fruited character at Full City and beyond, but that loss is made up for with intense bittersweetness and body. An absolute must for those after wilder, fruity single-origin espresso.</p> </div>
