<div class="column-right">
<p>The farm of Concepción Buena Vista is located in the region of Chimaltenango, right in between the city of Antigua and Lake Atitlan. With Volcan de Fuego bordering Chimaltenango, this tectonically active region has high altitude, and Buena Vista sits at right around 1700 meters above sea level. The farm has been a part of the Solano family for three generations, and grown from a small plot of land to the current 100 hectares, all planted in Bourbon. This is a fully washed coffee, and the coffee is carted down to Antigua where it is milled  and prepared for export at Bella Vista mill at the edge of town. We were taken a back by the balanced cup when roasted light and dark, the level of chocolate roast flavors in the middle roast ranges are second to none.</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>With a balance of bitter and sweet tones and moderate acidity level, this coffee from Buena Vista has the makings of a classic-toned espresso and crowd pleasing coffee. Flavors of bittering chocolate are balanced by sweet notes of buttery caramel and toffee. There's a lemony bright spot in the lighter roast levels (even C+) that, with an herbal accent, hints at lemon balm. Full City roasts color in hefty dark chocolate characteristics, milky body and a layered cocoa flavors in the finish. A rounded coffee at almost any roast level (up to Full City+) and makes a killer base for a cappuccino or other milk drinks too.</p> </div>
