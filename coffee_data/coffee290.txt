<div class="column-right">
<p>"Daurfusu" is the name of the farmer group in the Dukurai village community of Timor-Leste's Letefoho District. The group currently consists of 37 members, the elected "leader" of the group having had success in the 2019 Timor best of harvest competition, placing top ten overall. He has also taken on the role of educating the other famers on best practices and checks quality of all individual coffee lots submitted to the project, checking for visible inconsistencies and moisture levels of the incoming coffee. Farmers in this area are growing a mix of cultivars, mostly Timor Hybrid and Typica, at altitudes of 1400 to 1600 meters above sea level. Each farmer depulps and ferments their own coffee using locally made gum-wood and hammered tin de-pulpers. These are very small lots of coffee. Fermentation happens in buckets, rather than large tanks, and the coffee is dried on tarps outside.</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>I cupped this coffee next to a Brazil, which presented quite a contrast. Both are solid coffees. But going from a mild-toned Brazil to this Timor really helped to highlight this coffee's interesting, thought subtle, top notes. The aroma shows spiced and herbal accents atop smells of raw turbinado sugar. The cup has a similar sweet undercurrent, top notes of cinnamon, red bell pepper and lemon provide contrasting accents. A tangy characteristic comes up in lighter roasts that has a bit of a copper-like affect in mouthfeel. My Full City roast had intense bittering like cacao nibs. And while not the sweetest at this roast, the cup showed a unique peppery note that along with the smokey tones, presented a flavor that reminded me of a Sumatran-leaf cigar. </p> </div>
