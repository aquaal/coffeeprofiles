<div class="column-right">
<p>Keramo coffee is from a privately owned processing site near Keramo Village, tucked away in the Bombe mountains of Ethiopia's Sidama growing region. Along with coffee from their own farm called "Daye Bensa", they are buying and processing coffee from local farmers from three different growing areas nearby - Shantawene, Bombe and Keramo villages. The samples we tasted from all three village sites scored high and we bought wet and dry process lots from all of them (the others should be available early 2020). This lot is a mix of coffees harvested from their own farm and outgrowers around Keramo village. It's certified organic too, which means that they go to great lengths to register every small farmer who they buy from and certify that organic agricultural practices are being employed at the farm level (they're also Rain Forest Alliance and UTZ certified, but we only brought in with the organic certificate). The farm and outgrowers are growing Ethiopian heirlooms and altitude ranges from just under 2000 meters to 2100 meters above sea level. It's worth noting that the screen size is on the small side too. The majority of the coffee is 15+, but we are spotting quite a few 14 screen beans in there too. It's worth noting that we have another coffee available right now from the same group and neighboring village, "Sidama Bombe". The dry process lots from both stations were wildly popular when we featured them earlier this year.</p> </div>

<p>Sidama Keramo smells complex and clean, honey-like sweetness in both fragrance and aromatics, along with a perfumed floral note that is particularly prominent in the wet aroma. Sweet smells of maple, brown sugar, toffee, praline and more are raised in the steam, as well as an orange accent. At City roast level, the cup offers a delicious honey/orange mix, sweet and citrus notes flourish in the coffee as you move through it, opening up to accents of peach and nectarine, a jasmine note crowning the cup. While "orange" was one of the first fruit flavors I noted, Keramo doesn't have the high acidity you might expect from citrus-laden brew. It's rather mild for Ethiopia, but definitely not muted either. I'd characterize the acidic impression as being fruited or even like herbal lemon tea. I only roasted to City+ (somewhere between City+ and Full City really - about 2:30 past 1st Crack in a Quest, 28F development) but imagine the fruited qualities will hold up to Full City too. My City+ roast saw a boost in floral stone fruit flavors - fleshy peach, dried apricot and nectarine - as well as the beginnings of bittering roast tones. An exquisite Ethiopian pour-over.</p> </div>
