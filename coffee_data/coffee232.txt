<div class="column-right">
<p>The coffees that make up this lot are from three neighboring towns in the La Libertad Municipality in Huehuetenango - Paraiso, Peña Blanca and Bojonalito. The farms in La Libertad stretch up into the hillside villages that top out around 1800 meters. We know the three farmers whose coffees make up this lot well and try to stop by each year to see how the harvest is coming along and changes they may have implemented at their mills. The three farms are planted in a mix of cultivars, Bourbon, Caturra, Typica and Catuai and rest at altitudes ranging from 1600 to 1900 meters above sea level. Wet processing is managed at home with small, mechanized depulpers that feed into cement fermentation tanks. The coffee is "washed" and agitated in cement channels to remove the fruit after fermentation and then dried on cement patios. After drying the coffee down to about 11% moisture level, it is transported to a dry mill in Huehuetenango town where the parchment layer is removed, the coffee sorted, and then finally prepped for export. We spent many hours at the cupping table sorting through small producer lots from the region in order to put this 21 bag lot together. We use "Mejor" in the name because this coffee is made up of three of the highest scoring coffees from the La Libertad region this year.</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>At City roast level the dry fragrance has a scent of semi-refined sugar (without much of the molasses) and slight suggestions of dried fruit and herbal tea. The wetted crust lets off a much more potent sweetness that's saturated, rich with raw sugars. The brewed coffee is intensely sweet and levels a balanced cup no matter your roast preference. City and City+ roasts develop rounded sweetness with flavors of unrefined sugars, a little bit of roasted almond in the back end, as well as apple-like acidity that livens the cup. As the coffee cools, Mejor de Libertad gives off flavor notes that echo "desserts" like pecan pie, caramel flan and just a smidge of caramel-covered apple in light roasts. Dark chocolate bittersweets are intense at Full City and Full City+, low tones flourish, with a muted dried dark fruit flavor and clenching bittersweetness in the long finish. No matter where your roast winds up, Mejor de Libertad is a bodied cup with the weight of almond milk, and manages to pull out structured acidity in the lighter roasts without being the coffee's focal point. </p> </div>
