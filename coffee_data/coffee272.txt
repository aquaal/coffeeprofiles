<div class="column-right">
<p>This wet-hulled Aceh coffee is from a quality-savvy coffee collector named Auliya who buys in the districts of Bebesen and Kute Panang. Farms in these regions tend to span an altitude range of about 1300 to 1600 meters and are planted in older, long berry types (you'll see the long, canoe shaped beans) Abysinnia, Ateng and Tim Tim. In the scheme of things, Auliya's operation is quite small. He usually buys a few bags at a time, and then processes coffee with family at home. With small scale comes the capability for a high level of quality management, which he puts into all of the different process technics he employs (including full honey and dry process!). This year we paid a little extra for the coffee in order to help fund a drying room so that he can dry coffee inside on raised drying beds. </p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>A "classic" Aceh coffee character is found in the dry fragrance, slight fruit and sweet with molasses. It shows the foresty/peat moss aroma that is typical for wet-hull Aceh. But along with it comes a ribbon of dark cocoa, spiced with hints of cinnamon and nutmeg. The cup is an example of wet-hulled coffee done right - earth-toned sweetness comes off strong and complex, but the finish is devoid of dank, musty off-notes found in the average Sumatran coffee. The cup cools to complex cocoa flavors interspersed with an apple note and baking spice hints in the nose. Yes, the rustic qualities are there too, but kept well in balance with the rest. Acidity is present to accent the cup, but is very low in intensity, also typical for Sumatra.</p> </div>
