<div class="column-right">
<p>76 year old Agapito Rivera is still managing his small farm outside of Cajamarca with the help of his wife Petronila. A coffee farmer for most of his life, Agapito turned his focus to producing Specialty coffee some years back, trading his job as a dental technician to focus on the effort. He is a member of the Northern Peru producer group, Aromas del Valle, who represent small farmers from the towns of Jaen, Cutervo and San Ignacio. Now in their 6th year, Aromas del Valle are over 425 members strong and have developed a program to help provide economical and social support to producers and their families, as well as a focus on environmental sustainability that has led to organic certification (this is a certified lot). Farms range in altitude from 1700 to over 2000 meters above sea level.</p> </div>

<p>A complex fragrance is released in the dry grounds, with raw sugars and allusions to dried fruits. Fruited smells are a bit more vivid when smelling the wetted crust, hints of dried stone fruit and berry peeking through tightly-packed brown sugar sweetness. City roasts have a juicy quality in the cup with flavors of flame grape and plum emerging as the coffee cools. The sweetness comes off like simple syrup at City, transparent and clean. But as you pass over into City+ and Full City, the introduction of bittering roast tones interact with the underlying sweetness and constructs a flavor that's closer to caramelized sugars. The coffee beans are dense and will withstand high heat up front. You can develop rich bittersweetness by stretching roast times beyond the end of 1st crack without bringing out ashy flavors. In fact, our Full City roast had a super nice berry note along with dark chocolate notes that had a creamy sweetness.</p> </div>
