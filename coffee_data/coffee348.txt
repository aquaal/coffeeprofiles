<div class="column-right">
<p>Juan Espinoza is a small scale farmer in the Alto Pirias area of Peru's Chirinos district. You may recall the lot we had from another Espinoza family member, Graciela, a few weeks back. Juan is practically next door and has a similar set up in terms of coffee cultivars planted and production process. Alto Pirias is a small village in Chirinos, near to the larger village of Las Pirias. Like Juan and Graciela, there are a number of forward thinking producers in this region. Juan's farm is planted in Yellow Caturra, Catimor, Bourbon and Pache. His wet-processing technique involves leaving the whole coffee cherry overnight before depulping the following morning. It generally takes 24 to 36 hours of fermentation time to break down the sticky muculage enough to wash it away easily and move to the drying patios. Sometimes he ferments the coffee in sealed bags in order to impart more winey flavors in the cup, though this lot was not handled that way. You can see the dark yellow parchment layer in the 3rd photo which is due in part to this extended fermentation time.</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>This Peru micro lot from Juan Espinoza has a sweetness in the cup that just won't quit. Leading into the brewed coffee, the fragrance shows smells of brown sugar and cola soda, cane sugar and dark spices on into darker roasts too. The wet aromatics have warmed caramel and vanilla hints and frosting-like sweetness is raised in the steam. This is one sweet cup at a wide range of roasts. City+ roasts have caramel and toffee candied sweetenss that are accented by an apple flavor that is both tart and sweet, like apple skin and flesh. This coffee presents so well as a pour-over brew, with compact raw sugar sweetness, fruited acidity and finishes with a complex cinnamon-stick note. Full City roasts gush dark cocoa notes, some roasty in character like roasted nibs as well as a Dutch drinking cocoa flavor. The apple cup note tasted at City+ is still present but ultimately succumbs to the bittersweet roast tones.</p> </div>
