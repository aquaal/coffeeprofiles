<div class="column-right">
<p>Pulcal is an old coffee estate overlooking the colonial city of Antigua, and is situated at a range of 6000 feet in altitude. The farm dates to early colonial times (check out the neat tile crest in the first photo below), but coffee was planted in the late 1800's and first exported in 1918. The farm is said to have a unique micro-climate in the Antigua valley, as well as hillside exposure and well-draining soils. The farm is planted in old Typica and Bourbon cultivar trees, mostly red, but also a small amount of yellow fruit which gets separated out into a small lot each year - this year's totaling a whopping 8 bags! Most of the Yellow Bourbon is planted in the higher altitude part of the farm called "Inteligente", which due to altitude, the coffee tends to ripen in the middle harvest.</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>The Yellow Bourbon lot from Pulcal shows balance of sweet and bittering coffee flavors, and light top notes indicative of fine Central American coffees. The dry fragrance has a caramel sweetness with a note of almond paste/marzipan. The aroma of a City+ roast billowed a thick caramel smell in the steam off the wetted grounds, honey graham and cocoa tones that see quite a bump when taken to Full City. City roasts have mouthcleansing apple-like acidity on the heels of sweet, caramel-coated nut flavors. Fruited notes are subtle when the coffee is hot, but cool to a mild accent notes of apple and pear. This coffee is versatile in the roaster and Pulcal retains lively cup character even when roasted to Full City, where milk chocolate flavors take the form of chocolate milk or a cheap milk chocolate bar. </p> </div>
