<div class="column-right">
<p>We're calling this "Panameña Cultivar", but it is actually a mix of Panameña Gesha and Bourbon. A happy accident that means a much lower price point than 100% Gesha for this deliciously floral coffee. This is The picturesque town of Antigua is not far from Guatemala City, and sits in the shadow of Volcan de Fuego. Buena Vista is one of the farms owned by the Zelaya family, a family who for generations has farmed coffee in this region. Luis Pedro Zalaya Sr. and Jr. are still both very much involved in all stages of supply chain - from growing coffee at their farms in Antigua and Acatenango, to processing at their state of the art dry-mill, to exporting the final product. They do a great job at their dry mill, the physical quality of the green coffee looking near-pristine, which makes for a very even roast that is fairly easy to judge stages of development when roasting. This coffee should be roasted light in order to capture the floral cup character. It's not hard to acheive at City and City+, but roasting beyond this range will bring out balanced bittersweetness more than anything. </p> </div>

<p>This is a mix of Panameña Gesha and Bourbon grown at the Buena Vista farm in Antigua. The dry grounds of Buena Vista have a mild floral scent when roasted light, with a subtle fruited note and something like brown ale. It presents as "gesha" in fragrance and aroma, though more restrained than the flavor and aromatic highlights that come in the cup. At City roast level, this Gesha has the solid core bittersweetness that I'd expect from a fine Antigua coffee. It doesn't have the tea and delicate cup character like some of the other Gesha coffees, but rather, is bodied and sweet like Bourbon with mild floral jasmine flavors of a typical Gesha. Bittersweetness holds up to roast, however, the floral quality does not - and that's what makes this coffee a bit tricky to roast. Stick to City/City+ roasting if you're hoping for floral aromatics and you will be rewarded. That's where you'll tease out the jasmine floral quality in both flavor and aroma. The cup is marked by citrus accents, lime and lemon balm and a malty sweetness that returns to the "beer" characteristic sensed in the fragrance. </p> </div>
