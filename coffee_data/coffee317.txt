<div class="column-right">
<p>Bernardo Montoya's "Finca Las Aguacates" is located just outside the small town of Caicedo, Antioquia. It's not that far from Urrao, both areas sharing some of the same regional benefits: very high altitudes, natural shade from the surrounding mountain peaks, and plenty of fresh, cold water for processing. Las Aguacates is 4 hectares in total, half of which is planted almost entirely in Caturra and Variedad Colombia. Bernardo and his family are part of a small group of farmers in the area who are delivering their coffees to an intermediary we work with in the region, giving them and us the opportunity to separate out the top tier for a premium. Las Aguacates sits at about 1900 meters above sea level, and they have a small wet mill onsite where they wash and ferment their coffee, drying on raised beds inside a covered "parabolico" drying room. We spend the afternoon at Las Aguacates a few years back and Bernardo showed us the new parabolico he had built with the premium payment he received for the coffee we purchased that first year, along with two cows as well. He did a serious pruning too of about 20% of his coffee trees, the oldest on the farm, in order to foster new, healthy growth. Those are now in production and no doubt contributed to this lot we bought.</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>What I like about this lot from Bernardo Montoya is how versatile it is in the roaster. High grown coffees like this are dense and the tight cellular structure can take high heat application in the roaster. Regardless of where you wind up on the recommended roast spectrum (City to Full City+), you're sure to pull out a sweetness that helps balance the other cup flavors, from fruited to bittering cocoa, which are roast dependent. The smells have a woodsy spice hint at City, raw nut and subtle bittersweets. Light roasts produce a cup with simple brow sugar and accent notes of red bell pepper and chocolate almond. Full City+ roasts are heavily weighted in chocolate roast flavors, along with prune juice and herbaceous hints.</p> </div>
