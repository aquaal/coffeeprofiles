<div class="column-right">
<p>Ibutiti Estate is a privately owned farm by the Murathe family, located in the Kiria-ini area of Murang'a County. The Murathe's have roughly 40 hectares of coffee planted, mostly SL bourbon types. We had a chance to tour the farm and milling facility onsite last year, and they run an impressive operation. Their washing station is in good order, tidy and organized, as is their farm. The trees are well spaced and pruned, and appeared to be out-producing others we visited in the area, perhaps partly the result of agronomical care. This is the Peaberry outturn, and we also picked up an AA selection too. Both show incredibly impressive fruit flavors, with enough acidity to structure cup complexity. The physical quality of the green looks good too, with only a few semi-quakers spotted. This is a mere 4 bags, and at 93 points, won't last long.</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>Ibutiti's aroma packs a powerful punch with fruited characteristics at City and City+ roast levels, strong grape and spice smells in the steam, accents of dried orange peel and tropical fruits, cardamom and powdered ginger spice smells. Chocolate roast tones command attention at Full City and bring with them juicy dark fruit smells too. When brewing, flavor components of mulled grape juice come through across a pretty wide roast range. There's a juiciness in weight that along with a tangy aspect, conveys flavor aspects that find parity with pulpy orange juice. Fruit flavors really unfold as the coffee cools off, tropical notes of mango and papaya, tart cherry lemonade and a strong cranberry flavor. Complex spice notes seem to take over in the aftertaste and I'm left contemplating clove and all spice aromatics. Full City roasts are also very juicy, and dark fruits remain a focal point, bookended by heavy dark chocolate roast tones, along with a dried coconut note in the finish. Take your time moving through this Kenya as there are so many flavors to ponder.</p> </div>
