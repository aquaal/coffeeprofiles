<div class="column-right">
<p>Finca Rola is run by Marvin and Felipe Rodriguez in Naranjo micro region of Costa Rica's lush West Valley. The West Valley is a high altitude zone that sees coffee farms topping out much higher on average than those in the Central Valley. Finca Rola sits at a modest 1600 to 1700 meters above sea level and is planted mostly in Caturra trees. This coffee was processed at the famed Helsar de Zarcero mill, a name synonymous with the micromill revolution that happened back in the 1990s. We have had a buying relationship with Helsar de Zarcero mill, and the owning partner/manager Ricardo Perez, for close to 15 years now. Each year we see quality refinements to their process, with new drying beds, warehouse space and dry mill equipment being added in the past season. And we see a consistent quality in that comes from their processing methods, a clean-tasting, classic Costa Rica character with both brightness and balance. This is one of a handful of coffees we bought from Helsar during the 2019/20 harvest season.</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>This coffee from Finca Rola brews a crowd-pleasing cup at a broad roast range, promising sweetness and brew-versatility at both middle and dark roast levels. Grinding a City+ roast releases brown sugar sweetness with accents of walnut and butterscotch chips and reminds me of blond brownie batter. The wet aromatics are potent with buttery caramel and sugar browning smells. City+ roasts show a simple sweetness in the hot brewed coffee like light brown sugar without much molasses, and cools to something more complex like vanilla caramels, candied walnut and a coffee-flavored hard candies. My Full City+ roast that went a full 10 seconds into 2nd Crack had a delicious cacao chocolate bar flavor when hot that opened up to more nuanced cocoa flavors like tootsie roll, chocolate torte and smoked chocolate dessert. This should definitely be on your short list of single origin espressos.</p> </div>
