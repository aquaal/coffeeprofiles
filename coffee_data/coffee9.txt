<div class="column-right">
<p>Kiandu Factory is located near Nyeri Town in the Karundu area, just outside of Tetu village. When we say "Factory", we mean washing station more or less. Kenyan coffee factories are central coffee collection sites set up to serve the local farmers. Factory members sell their harvested coffee to the Factory they belong to in whole cherry where it is then sorted, wet processed, and then sorted some more while drying in preparation for the final dry milling. Kiandu is one of a few Factories who make up the Mutheka Farmer's Cooperative Society, and the total membership of the society is over 1000 farmers. Kiandu is situated near the Kagumo River, from which water is diverted and used to process coffee. This AB separation stood out to us, boasting floral characteristics that we don't typically taste in Kenyan coffee. We found our lighter roasts showed complex baking spice notes that worked well with citrus and other fruited hints. City/City+ roasts are fantastic as pour over, and the syrupy, dark sweetness of Full City works great for full immersion brew methods too.</p>
<p>*Coffee ships in vacuum sealed boxes from origin - please note, the vacuum seal may break in transit but the coffee is still protected</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>Orange spice and dried fruit smells in Kiandu follow through from the first whiffs of ground coffee to the flavors in a cooling cup. Spiced fruit aspects are especially prominent in City and City+ roasts, complimented by a level of sweetness that seems well developed, a pungent caramelizing sweetness with fruited accents. In the cup, fruited notes take the form of black cherry, a dark fruited sweetness with complimentary puckering mouthfeel, like the cherry fruit closest to the skin. Kiandu is intense brewed across a wide roast range. A sweet orange accent that pops up makes for a lively cup characteristic at City roast, and Full City pushes syrupy dark fruit flavors like pomegranate and grape, along with a pungent sugar-browning bittersweetness. The cooling cup exhibits quite the assemblage of spiced accent notes like clove powder, cardamom and all-spice in the nose. The perceived acidity adds a sharp contrast to sweetness like the tangy side of a pink grapefruit, and particularly vibrant at City roast level.</p> </div>
