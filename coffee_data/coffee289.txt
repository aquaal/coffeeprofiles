<div class="column-right">
<p>This coffee is the product of a newly-started coffee cherry buying operation in the Haupu tribal region of Timor Leste. The project was set in motion by Australian native Peter Dougan who developed a fresh farm produce program in East Timor a few years back. He's since added coffee to the mix, partly as a way to boost farmer's incomes. A wet mill was erected on land owned by a local leader and the buying operation began. The wet mill is called "Lutlala", also the name of the hamlet within which it located, and is owned by a local leader. They are mainly buying coffee from three surrounding growing areas which span an altitude range of 1300 - 1500 meters above sea level: Dukurai, Goulolo, and Eraulo. They're also producing natural, wet and honey process coffees, all of which we picked up this year!</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>This natural is quite a departure from some of the wet process Timors we've carried. The fruited smells in the dry fragrance and wet aroma have a sort of strawberry milk powder scent with mild grapefruit-like citrus at light roast levels, a bit more like dehydrated or cooked berries in middle roasts. The cup is fruited at a range of roasts, sweetness somewhat unrefined and with mild acidity. Our City roast hinted at baked apple and pear pastries, dried strawberry and rustic palm sugar sweetene that lends an earthy flavor tone to the cup. City+ roasts layer on bittering cocoa powder and dark chocolate flavor notes, and a somewhat rustic fruited flavor of red berries flourish. I didn't roast this coffee darker than City+, but imagine it will retain some of the berry characteristics, and no doubt will yield a chocolatey brew too.</p> </div>
