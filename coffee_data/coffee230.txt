<div class="column-right">
<p>"Marca Roja" refers to the town where these coffees are from in the La Libertad district of Huehuetenango. This 56 bag lot is made up of coffees from 7 different producers, all of which we've bought coffee from the past several years now. You mostly see cultivar types like Bourbon, Caturra, Catuaí and Typica and the altitude ranges from around 1600 to 1900 meters above sea level. We bring this coffee in as part of our Xinabajul project in Huehuetenango. The goal is to find small farmers who are producing great coffee and pay them a premium price that they deserve. We hope to work with many more families throughout the Huehuetenango region, helping to improve resources when we can and ultimately the value of the coffee they produce. </p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>The dry fragrance has a nice malt sugar and cinnamon spice scent, sweet and clean, reminiscent of snickerdoodle cookies. Wetting the grounds with hot water brings out caramelized sugar sweetness, also presenting a confectionary slant, like caramel and brown sugar candies. At City+ the brewed coffee has creamy body, a nice weighty mouthfeel that helps to convey a caramel flavor and milky texture of the liquor. The level of sweetness rates really high on our cupping graph (9.0!) and proved to be resilient to roast. The caramel-y, toffee-like sweet flavors that are prominent at City+ are hardly affected by darker roasting, at least up to Full City+. Roast tones are more pronounced, of course, but these hefty chocolate flavors find their match in underlying sweetness. This may not be the most complex Guatemalan coffee. But what it lacks in top notes it makes up for in balance and sweetness! Highly recommended for versatility and just a coffee I'd like to drink more of.</p> </div>
