<div class="column-right">
<p>This coffee is from the Bonilla family's 'Don Mayo' micro mill. This is one of the oldest of the micro mills, starting back in 2003, and Hector Bonilla has worked at perfecting production at the facility along with his sons. This particular lot is from La Ladera, a farm in Leon Cortes de Tarrazu, and planted almost entirely in Caturra cultivar. They use a Penagos for mechanically-washing at the station (a version of pulp-natural that closely replicates actual 'wet processing'), an efficient water conserving machine. After soaking in clean water, the wet parchment is moved to raised beds to facilitate even drying from above and below. They are also equipped with their own dry-milling equipment and able to peel the parchment layer from the seed as well as sort the coffee by density. That's part of the beauty of Familia Bonilla/Don Mayo mill, is their ability to oversee the coffee processing from the time it is harvested to the milled green coffee. Like many of the micro-mills, Don Mayo processes coffee from their own farms, as well as buys whole cherry from local farmers to sell as individual micro-lots.</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>Toffee sweetness and roasted almond accent La Ladera's aroma, like the sweet scent of almond paste and pecan short bread cookies. City+ roasting plays up raw sugar and nut tones in the brewed coffee, yielding flavors of toffee-nut and a molasses-drizzled almonds. A fruited accent emerges when the cup cools off some, but are off in the background compared to the rest. I noted "golden raisin" when cup testing, but only in my lightest roast (City+). Full City has balanced chocolate roast flavors that have aspects of layered chocolate wafer cookies. La Ladera shows best in the City+ to Full City roast range and this type of cup profile performs really well in full-immersion brew methods like French Press, or try the new <a href="https://www.coffeeshrub.com/cupping-brewer.html">Rattleware Cupping Brewer</a> (no, it's not just for cupping!). Full City and Full City+ roasts are guaranteed to yield deep, dark chocolate flavors as espresso too.</p> </div>
