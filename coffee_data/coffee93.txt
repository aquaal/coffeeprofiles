<div class="column-right">
<p>Foge is the name of the small town, or "Kebele", where this privately owned processing site is located. It's very close to Yirga Cheffe town and coffee is pulled from small farms throughout the region. The group who manage this milling site have several other processing sites throughout Yirga Cheffe and Guji, some of which we've brought in under the names "Gedeb", "Arusi", etc. In general, the name is borrowed from the location and there are often many other processing sites nearby that use the same name. For the customer, this makes it seem like these coffees are everywhere and the same. To add another layer of confusion, even if they are from the exact same site, process batches can vary wildly from one to the next, influenced by factors such as weather, harvest selection, time of the season, how loaded the drying beds are and more. The takeaway is that not all coffee sold as "Foge", "Gedeb", "Konga" or any of the other commonly used names will cup the same and might even be from different washing stations. The Foge site is surrounded by incredibly high altitude peaks and the farmers they are buying cherry from have coffee planted as high as 2100 meters above sea level. This is our last Yirga Cheffe coffee until new crop arrives.</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>Our roast that just barely made it through 1st crack proved to be be the most floral, which is of course what we hope for in a nice Yirga Cheffe! And this one is certainly that. The aroma is perfumed with bergamot and dried citrus peel, honey suckle flower and sugar-cookie dough. This is a light-bodied coffee, appropriate for the delicate City roast cups in my opinion. Flavors of honey and citrus-spritzed water have a light touch, as do jasmine flower aromatics. Acidity comes off like mild citrus too and has the refreshing feel of sweet lime. Roasting closer to City+ lends a little bit more heft to the profile but at the expense of the volatile aromatics that won us over. Don't get me wrong, City+ roasts still produce a delicious cup. But there's a little less floral and citrus, and more of a honey/malted barley profile here. I will say the sweetness shows the best balance at this roast level, but will ultimately go back for the lighter City myself.</p> </div>
