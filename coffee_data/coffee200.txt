<div class="column-right">
<p>This coffee is from the family of Daniel Perez who have a sizable family-run farm just outside of Huehuetenango town. The farm spans an altitude range of 1500 - 1700 meters above sea level and planted in a mixture of older cultivar types. This is a lot separation of Pacamara, which is a hybrid of Maragoype and Pacas, phenotypically identifiable by its large bean size. It's not quite as big as Maragoype, but still presents a minor challenge to my burr grinder! They can also be a bit trickier to roast and I recommend letting the roast roll for an additional 15-30 seconds beyond the point at which you think you've reached your roast target. Similar to Maracaturra and Maragogype, the large beans need a little extra time in the roaster for internal roast development. This is a densely sweet coffee and produces a convincing acidity level when roasted North of Full City (City+ for my review). Only 20 bags available.</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>When grinding my City+ roast a smell of butter brickle ice cream comes to mind. The coffee has a buttery sweetness in both fragrance and wet aromatics that hints at roasted nut as well, accent notes bringing to mind maple drizzled almond and a contrasting licorice note. Brewing middle roasts you're presented with flavors similar to the aromatic impressions. Deep, brown sugar flavors and clean, roasted nut accent notes strike near-perfect balance. Flavor notes of butterscotch, milk chocolate and roasted almond line the cup, an accent note of almond oil hangs on in the aftertaste. Acidity is a bit softer than what I recall in last year's lot, but still manages to add an impression of black tea to the coffee. Full City roasts show heavy chocolate roast tones that are rivaled by an undercurrent of raw sugary sweetness. This is about the cut off for me in terms of roast development and still yields a balanced cup. </p> </div>
