<p>You may be familiar with the name "Los Palomos" because of the small lots we've purchased from the Urrao coffe farm we've purchased in past years. This is the sister farm in the south in Inzá, Cauca, also owned by Leonardo Henao and managed by his good friend Robinson Pillimué. Leo's worn many hats in his years of coffee work including farmer outreach, studying agronomy and fermentation at University, handling logistics for coffee exporters, and managing his own farms name a few. Palomos del Sur was already planted in coffee when Leo bought the land, mostly Variedad Colombia and Castillo. Since acquisition, Leo and Robinson have planted several thousand new coffee trees, including Caturra, Bourbon, Gesha and others. The farm is located in Vereda Agua Blanca and altitude runs a range from 1750 to about 1900 meters above sea level.</p> </div>

<p>The dry fragrance of Palomos del Sur has herbal-sweet smells at City+ roast level, raw sugar and subtle earth-toned accents. The wet aroma has a complex molasses smell, with accents of date and pine resin escaping with the steam when breaking through the wetted crust. At City+ the sweetness in the brewed coffee has a pungent, caramelizing sugar flavor, accented by hints of berry and fresh tarragon leaf in the finish. Fruits don't factor in too heavily in the cup, but I pick up on a flavor that reminds me of cooked plantain. Darker roasting develops deeper low tones, like roasted cacao nibs and baking cocoa. Acidity comes through in light and middle roasts, though still on the moderate side overall.</p> </div>
