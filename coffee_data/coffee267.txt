<div class="column-right">
<p>This coffee is the result of an ambitious project that started almost a decade ago, to build an outpost in the oldest coffee growing region in Java, work with the local farmers, and process the coffee in tiny batches to high standards. Java Sunda (West Java) was the original coffee area, but you would find very, very few trees here when this project began. Most of the coffee was grown in East Java at this time, where all the big estates are. But farmers in Java Sunda always kept small coffee plots, and there was one commodity quality mill near Bandung that would buy coffee (but not for much). Yet here among the Ateng and Jember were some old Typica trees, the original Typica! (Java was the first destination for coffee from Yemen, with a stopover in India). This small wet-hulled ("giling basah") lot is from the Kelompok farmers group in the Garut area. It is on the slopes of the Papandayan volcano, with coffee planted from 1400-1550 meters. All the coffee cherry is floated before processing to remove under and overripe coffee, only allowing the densest beans to pass for Grade 1.</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>Palm sugar, earthy sweetness, body and mild acidity, this wet-hulled coffee from Java brings to mind classic, earth-toned Sumatran cup. The aroma at City+ smells of rustic sweeteners and a fragrant palo santo/wood-spice. Full City roast level is a good place to start and highlights a rustic cocoa flavor note that reminds me of roasted cacao nibs. Bittersweetness helps to balance out the woodsy cup characteristics and rustic sweetness, acting as a sort of mortar to aromatic notes of peat, leather and eucalyptus. The cup is herbal, earthy and complex and easily supplants wet-hulled Sumatras in a blend or enjoyed on its own. Fans of Sumatran coffees will really enjoy this one.</p> </div>
