<div class="column-right">
<p>This coffee is from a washing station in the Huye Mountains of Rwanda, "Ibisi Bya Huye" being an old local name for this high elevation region. The washing station acts as a buying site where the small-scale farmers in this highland community can sell their coffee cherry during the harvest. The Huye Mountain Coffee Washing Station is connected with the global market through a Rwandan exporter we buy from each year, which is how we came upon the original set of offer samples from Ibisi a few years back. Bourbon is still the cultivar farmers grow in this area, which no doubt plays a role in this coffee's supremely sweet cup profile. Altitude at the station is right around 2000 meters above sea level, and the farms are on the +/- side of this number, but not by much. This is a peaberry selection from the washing station, the small, round bean that occurs when one of the two coffee embryos dies, leaving behind a single bean that takes on the shape of the outer cherry. They tend to be small, about 15 1/64th inch, round, and quite dense. </p> </div>

<p>Honey and spice are what I pick up on the smells from light roasted, ground coffee. It's sweet, delicate to some degree and with mild pear and citrus notes. The wet aroma has a smell of  sweet baked goods like bran muffin with raw sugars and a dusting of cinnamon filling things out. City roasts are vibrant, citric even, and open up to complex spice, raw sugar and citrus flavor notes. Lemon and orange flavors accent a somewhat transparent simple syrup sweetness and a lively quality through the finish. Tea notes and spiced hints come through as it cools - kind of like Earl Grey and lemongrass teas flavor in terms of spice notes - and really comes through in the finish as you exhale. I loved drinking this light roast as pour over and found the cup to have a lovely tart-sweetness at it's core. Full City+ roasts aren't too shabby either and smokey bittersweetness gives way to a thin layer of dark grape. I wish I'd roasted a batch to Full City too in order to try dialing back the 'smokey' roast flavor and bringing out the fruited undertone. There's always next time!</p> </div>
