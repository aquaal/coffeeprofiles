<div class="column-right">
<p>This coffee is the product of a newly-started coffee cherry buying operation in the Haupu tribal region of Timor Leste. The project was set in motion by Australian native Peter Dougan who developed a fresh farm produce program in East Timor a few years back. He's since added coffee to the mix, partly as a way to boost farmer's incomes. A wet mill was erected on land owned by a local leader and the buying operation began. The wet mill is called "Lutlala", also the name of the hamlet within which it located, and is owned by a local leader. This lot comes from farms near the village of Mobere in the Ermera District, near the slopes of mount Ramelau. The farms span a range of 1500 - 1700 meters above sea level and you mostly see Timor hybrids and Typica cultivars in this region. The coffee is fermented for 24 - 48 hours depending on the weather, sent to drying tables, and then once the coffee is sold it is driven to a dry mill in Dili for final preparation. They're also producing natural, wet and honey process coffees, all of which we picked up this year. However, this is the final Timor coffee of the season!</p> </div>

<p>The dry grounds have a clean scent of raw sugars, caramel and butterscotch, a sweet smelling coffee. The wet aroma shows a fruited hint at City roast level that alludes to dried apple, but is mostly made up of caramelized sugar and bittersweet smells. Sweetness and roast bittering flavors are balanced in the brew and it's one of our brighter, more dynamic Timor's that puts it on a different level than some of the others. If you put this side by side with one of our more basic Guatemalas, I'd believe they were from the same origin. The sweetness is caramel-y and delicious, offset by bittering notes of Dutch cocoa and a subtle cooked fruited hint in the finish once cooled. At City+ and Full City roast levels, Mobere is very much a crowd pleasing brew, one that I enjoy on its own, but that will stand up to cream if you prefer (definitely at Full City). This is our last Timor-Leste coffee for the season and certainly taking us out on a high note.</p> </div>
