<div class="column-right">
<p>Finca Acopio Suyatal comes to us from Dipilto, a region within the larger Nueva Segovia Department. Nueva Segovia sits along the northern border of Nicaragua, very close to Honduras, and offers some of the most ideal climatic regions for coffee production in Nicaragua. At 1500 meters, Acopio Suyatal is one of the highest altitude farms of the small suite of estates run by Cafetalera Buenos Aires. The farm is roughly 10 manzanas in size and planted in mostly Caturra with some Maragogype shrubs peppered throughout. This is a small dry process, all Caturra cultivar lot separation. These micro lots are given special attention. The cherry is floated to remove under and overripe cherries, then laid to dry on raised African beds. The cherry is then hand picked over the time it takes to dry on the beds, generally around 3 weeks. At 1200 meters, Finca Buenos Aires is a somewhat low farm compared to other origins we buy from. Yet this farm produces some of our best Nicaraguan lots each year. </p> </div>

<p>A distinct berry smell is released when grinding the coffee, along with a scent of green tree nuts like pistachio. The wet aroma is less fruited and expresses toffee sweetness with a note of brown sugar and cinnamon. The cup is fruit-forward at a wide range of roasts, berry highlights being the most obvious characteristic. The fruited qualities are like dried fruit and fruit jam, and if you've tasted the wet-process version of Suyatal, this lot illustrates how different coffee can taste depending on how the coffee was processed. The cup is weighty in texture and lends to flavor notes of strawberry milk and berry sauce, which lead into tree nut notes and a fruit toffee flavor in the finish. Full City roasts have an appealing bittersweet roast flavor that compliments deep, dark fruit tones and equates to a delicious darker roasted brew.</p> </div>
