<div class="column-right">
<p>Genji Challa is the sister site to Nano Challa, a cooperative that produces some of our most highly anticipated Western Ethiopian coffees each year. It's only 3km from the Nano Challa site, and between them, their membership has grown from 500 last year to more than 650 coop members for the 2019/20 harvest season. Doubling up wet mills allows the coop to better serve current members by growing their capacity as well as increase their ability to reach more farmers in the region. They've recently started producing dry process coffee too. In the past, we've only bought their delicious wet process lots (and have one on the way right now). But after tasting this fruit-forward, sweet natural coffee we just couldn't pass it up. The whole coffee cherries are floated in water first to remove any of the "floaters" - underripe coffee that is lower quality - before being moved to raised beds to finish drying over the course of two weeks, give or take. The typical black netting you see wrapped around the tops of the drying tables are covered with jute bags in order to help keep the surface from absorbing heat while the coffee is drying. Both stations sit at just under 1900 meters, and the farm members have coffee as high as 2000 meters above sea level. The coop was formed as part of an initiative to aid farmers in a more comprehensive way, but have since "graduated" from this program and are now part of an independent, local Union who assist the cooperatives with functions like marketing, exporting, and general representation on an international scale.</p> </div>

<div>This dry process lot from the Genji Challa cooperative produces some of the stone fruit cup flavors I associate with coffee from Nano Challa (the sister site, you may be familiar with), but with layers of chewy dried fruits that hint at berry and much more. The aromatics are sweet and with some rustic dry process notes like fruit pulp and berry ferment. The coffee smells very sweet, intense with flavors that evoke cherry filling, fruit jams and yellow peach. Flavors of rustic sweeteners are part of the package, a sort of molassesy sweetness that leads into natural dried apricot in the finish. At City roast level stone fruit-like flavors underscore a vibrant impression, which is a nice surprise, as dry process coffees are often flat and far from 'lively'. Glimpses of berry fruit leather and soft floral hints can be found in the cup aroma as you move through it. Some of these flavors may be easier to parse out from a cupping spoon, however, pour over brewing still yields an intensely fruited coffee. A lot of the dry process cup characteristics are well-integrated into the coffee's sweetness.</div> </div>
