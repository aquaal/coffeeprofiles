<div class="column-right">
<p>This honey process Aceh coffee is from a quality-savvy coffee collector named Auliya who buys in the districts of Bebesen and Kute Panang. Farms in these regions tend to span an altitude range of about 1300 to 1600 meters and are planted in older, long berry types (you'll see the long, canoe shaped beans) Abysinnia, Ateng and Tim Tim. In the scheme of things, Auliya's operation is quite small. He usually buys a few bags at a time, and then processes coffee with family at home. With small scale comes the capability for a high level of quality management, which he puts into all of the different process technics he employs (including full honey and dry process!). This year we paid a little extra for the coffee in order to help fund a drying room so that he can dry coffee inside on raised drying beds. </p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>We've picked up a few honey lots from Auliya over the past two years, and this one is a lot less fruit-forward than some of the others. It has a sweet spice characteristic and herbaceaus aromatics and dark cocoa accents that are intensified with roast development. The sweetness in the cup is nuanced, the range of flavor characteristics goes from simple brown sugar to the sweetness found in Tamari sauce. The coffee is sweet in the front and leads in to pungent, herbal and spice hints that are capped off by a bittersweet finish. Body acts as a sort of allusion to "fruit" in that it is quite juicy in texture. City+ roasts are marked by safron accents and a flavor of dark, black tea in the middle and finish. Bittersweetness and body will surely develop as you head toward darker roast territory.</p> </div>
