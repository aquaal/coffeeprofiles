<div class="column-right">
<p>The coffee comes from small holder coffee farmers in the Goro Badessa Woreda of Hambela Wamena, a high altitude region that tops out at 2100 meters above sea level. These lots are made up of coffee from several hundred different farmers, most with only a couple hundred coffee trees or less. You tend to see coffee intercropped with other fruits and vegetables, "false banana" being one the more common food staples to see planted in the Hambela region. The false banana plant has many uses, and is widely utilized for it's starchy inners that are often fermented with yeast to make a bread ("kocho"), and the leaves can be transformed into roofs for houses, baskets, and more. The Goro coffee is processed in a nearby town at a privately run wet mill, where they process both washed and natural coffees. This lot underwent several layers of hand picking from the whole coffee cherry to the final milled green coffee in order to achieve "Grade 1" status. The mill that produced this lot used to have to sell their coffee to the Ethiopia Commodities Exchange, as export licenses were only granted to cooperatives and coffee Unions. 2 years ago the government loosened these laws considerably. Now, the plethora of privately-run coffee stations and smaller estate farmers are able to sell their coffee directly to buyers, retaining provenance, rather than having their coffee sold by the government exchange as a generic, regional blend ("Guji Grade 1", "Yirgacheffe Grade 3", etc).</p> </div>

<p>Goro presents potent aromatics and intense fruited character in light to middle roasts, just how we like our dry process Ethiopias! The dry fragrance is peppered with berry notes both dried and fresh, accented by hints of fruit punch and brandy. The wet aroma has a fragrant floral hint, cranberry sauce and alluring baking spice accents. City roasts have some tartness to the fruited flavor profile, which lends an acidic impression to the cup flavors. Overall, I wouldn't call Goro "bright" by any stretch of the word, but lower acidity is part of the trade off in most dry process coffees, and we get the wild fruited coffee flavors instead. Top notes in light roasts include hints of raspberry jam and strawberry taffy, dark dried fruits, licorice and a subtle jasmine aroma. Full City roasts have immense body, which pairs well with a delicious flavor of dried blueberry covered in dark chocolate and a hint of anise in the aftertaste.</p> </div>
