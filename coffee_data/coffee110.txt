<div class="column-right">
<p>One thing is for sure, Gatugi's cup shows great saturation of sweetness that covers a lot of ground in terms of sugar types depending on roast level. The dry grounds are aroma show some sugar-browning sweetness at City and City+ that smells like caramelizing sugars in a pan, kind of a creme brûlée crust appeal. I don't pick up on a lot of fruit in the smells other than traces of dried fruit like raisin. The cup is very sweet and some of the flavors in my City roast reminded me of crepe toppings - brown sugar, maple, lemon and a hint of powdered sugar in aroma. The coffee is bright, citric and with a succinct, clean finish. Layers of sweetness are peeled back as it cools revealing hints of panela, sugar in the raw, and a perfumed sugar cane juice note. My dark roast attempt was beyond Full City but not quite into 2nd Crack (35F of development from the beginnings of 1st Crack) and the cup was still so sweet. The sharp acidity of City roasts is whittled down quite a bit when roasted dark, and roast development brings out blackberry and plum-like accent notes. This one is on my list for espresso to try at the end of the week.</p> </div>
