He purchases wet parchment from the surrounding farmers, mostly sigarar utang variety. "}'>Parapat Village lies on the east short of Lake Toba, a district called Sipangan Bolon. This part of North Sumatra is a ways away from Aceh, where many of our Sumatran coffees are from, but the flavor profile is quite similar to the Aceh coffee. Expect herbal-to-rustic sweetness, earth tones, big body and generally an extremely complex cup.This coffee was purchased through a coffee collector near Parapat who buys from a few different farmer groups in the region.   This particular lot comes from a group of 360 farmers in the hilly outskirts of Parapat who are growing mostly S795 and Typica. Altitude ranges from 1150 to 1200 meters. This lot struck us as having pronounced acidity for Sumatran coffee, though I wouldn't categorize it as "bright". Still, it adds a level of structure missing in some others.</span></p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>This Northern Sumatra coffee shares many cup characteristics with the classic, herbal-toned coffees of Aceh. The dry fragrance has a resin-like sweetness, rustic but nicely potent. The wet aroma has forest-y smells, pine sap, cedar shakes and the like. In the brew, this coffee from Parapat village shows surprising vibrance for a wet-hulled coffee. I wouldn't call it "bright", but it does have a orange rind flavor that offers a bright spot amidst strong earth tones, licorice root and a note of peat in the finish. The flavor profile unfolds as is it cools and is as complex as any of our other Sumatran coffees we had this year. Full City roasts unlock smokey chocolate roast flavors that are accented by notes of pipe tobacco, burdock, basil and chocolate-malted grains. </p> </div>
