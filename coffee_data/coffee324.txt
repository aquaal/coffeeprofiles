<div class="column-right">
<p>Rio Combeima runs North from Ibagué, the canyon it's carved out splits the mountain in two. The two coffee communities that make up the Combeima blend - Machín and China Alta - occupy the east and west sides of this river and are hubs for a new Tolima buying project that we are a part of. The intermediary we work with operate in the area by meeting with local farmers, visiting farms and process sites, and of course, tasting samples. They offer to pay farmers a premium for the opportunity to separate out coffee that meet a particular quality target, coffee that would otherwise be sold off for much lower, base prices and blended away as large Excelso blends by the local associations. Altitude in both coffee towns spans a range of 1850 and 1950 meters above sea level and the cultivars typically grown are Caturra and Variedad Colombia. Some of the farmers are mixing batches from up to a few days in the fermentation tanks, changing the water with each day's addition in order to keep the coffee from over fermenting. This is part of the reason the coffee is fruit forward for a wet-process coffee.</p> </div>

<p>A fragrance of candied nut and caramel escapes the ground coffee. I picked up on subtle dehydrated fruit notes too, but nothing so overt. The fruited smells are more pronounced in wet aroma and fold into brown sugar sweetness with a smell of fruit cake. Combeima makes a delicious cup at City+, raw sugar sweetness with molasses hints give way to a flavor note of green apple. Mouthfeel is a little tannic like iced tea and accompanied by a mild citrus-like acidic impression. There's an herbal aspect that comes out in the finish as the coffee cools down, adding to a green bell pepper note in the aroma and accenting a plum wine finishing flavor. </p> </div>
