<div class="column-right">
<p>This coffee is from Oscar Segura Vasquez, a farmer with little more than a single hectare of land planted in coffee. His coffee sits just outside the village of El Paraiso and the land slopes upward from 1700 meters all the way up to 2015 meters above sea level. Oscar is growing a few different cultivars, but has reserved the highest altitude section for only Caturra. That's what this lot is made up of; an all Caturra separation from the 2000+ meter part of his farm. Being such a small farm Oscar and his family handle all of the picking and processing responsibilities themselves, managing depulping in a hand-crank depulper, fermenting for a day and a half and then drying for upwards of 10 days on a cement patio. The group who set up the Huabal buying operation are working to register all the farmers they buy from in order to help isolate different quality tiers, but also be able to issue the initial payments and any quality premiums themselves, something that was lacking when they were buying through more traditional avenues. It doesn't help that the roads leading to Huabal and within the district are much more rustic than some of their neighbors, limiting access to farmers in this region.</p> </div>

<p>Bitter and sweet smells in the dry grounds are like honey-laced chocolate bar, with a dried banana hint. The wet aroma manages to display fruited accent notes at City+ as well, but most notable are sweet notes of honey and molasses. If I had to put a word to this Peru's sweetness I think it would have to be "muscovado", an aromatic, unrefined sugar with the molasses intact. There's a top note of apple at City and CIty+ roast levels, the "apple" aspect really coming through in aroma and impacting the cup . Acidic impressions are a big part of the cup when roasted light and bring really nice structure to the cup profile. I have to confess that after enjoying my City roast for a couple of days, and then running out, I started brewing my Full City and may have enjoyed it more! "Chocolate", check. "Body", check. Those I expected. But the berry note that accompanies these characteristics is almost as pronounced as the rest. Delicious under both roast applications and I'm faced with a difficult decision next time I roast it!</p> </div>
