<div class="column-right">
<p>This dry process coffee is from a quality-savvy coffee collector named Auliya who buys in the districts of Bebesen and Kute Panang, in Sumatra's Aceh province. Farms in these regions tend to span an altitude range of about 1300 to 1600 meters and are planted in older, long berry types (you'll see the long, canoe shaped beans) Abysinnia, Ateng and Tim Tim. In the scheme of things, Auliya's operation is quite small. He usually buys a few bags at a time, and then processes coffee with family at home. With small scale comes the capability for a high level of quality management, which he puts into all of the different process technics he employs (including full honey and dry process!). This year we paid a little extra for the coffee in order to help fund a drying room so that he can dry coffee inside on raised drying beds. Only 10 bags in total of this uniquely processed Sumatra!</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>The dry grounds certainly display fruited characteristics, as well as a sugar caramelization smell that has a sort of savory sweetness to it, like caramel and balsamic. The aroma smells strong, pungent spice and fruit hints are like prune and dark berry. In the cup I pick up on a lot of raw cocoa in my darkest roast, which was near Full City+. The bittering flavors are potent and carry a fruited side producing a flavor that reminds me of Scharffenberger chocolate drink. Dark roasts are delicious and give way to subtle herbal accent notes as the coffee cools down in temp. Body is silky which carries the chocolate flavors nicely. Don't be afraid of roasting light either, as dry process, fruited cup characteristics flourish alongside top notes like orange marmalade and freeze dried raspberry. I should note that light roasts convey some of the earthy flavors you might expect from Sumatra, that along with the fruity dry process notes, I can best describe as tasting a little like beet juice. A complex coffee no matter the roast level.</p>
