<div class="column-right">
<p>Herber Chali's farm is located in the region of Chimaltenango, right in between the city of Antigua and Lake Atitlan. The mountainous foothills equates to good altitude, his farm ranging from 1600 - 1700 meters above sea level. Don Herber is growing mainly Bourbon, Caturra, and some Typica varietals. The coffee is carted down to Antigua where it is milled at Bella Vista, the folks who handle the majority of our Antigua coffees (Pulcal, Cabrejo, etc). We keep going back to Herber's coffee as it's consistently balanced in terms of core bittersweetness and with top note highlights providing contemplative accents notes (especially in the lighter roasts). It's also versatile in the roaster which makes it a good candidate for home roasting machines that have less manual controllability, like a popper for example, or roasters where you don't have physical access to the coffee to judge roast color like the Behmor. It's pretty difficult to disrupt this coffee's balance no matter the roast level!</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>Caramel sweetness is pervasive in the dry fragragrance, and something perfumed comes through in small measure. The wet aroma sees a subtle spice note lingering in the steam amidst a dominant sweet smell of caramel sauce. At City, the brewed coffee displays raw sugar and caramel like sweetness and accented by mild bittering cocoa powder and a creamy nut aroma (think Macadamia or Brazil nut). This is a balanced coffee at a broad range of roast levels and sure to be crowd pleasing too. There's a slight red fruit impression too that add a sort of black plum or prune undertone to this balanced, sweet-to-bitter coffee. Full City roasts bolster the low tones and pushing chocolate bittersweetness way out front. The brew will leave those lovers of big bodied, 'chocolatey' coffees satisfied, and also serves up nicely as delicious espresso too.</p> </div>
