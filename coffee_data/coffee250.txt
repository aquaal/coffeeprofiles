<div class="column-right">
<p>Finca Buenos Aires Estates is a fairly large farm of about 215 hectares in the Dipilto growing region of Nueva Segovia. On our visits to Buenos Aires mill we cup through small batches from the farm, and have been impressed with the clean sweetness of their cultivar-specific process batches. It's really no surprise the they earn regular placement in the Cup of Excellence competitions. This one is all Caturra, which is a cultivated mutation of Bourbon that occurred in Brazil in the mid '30s, but was planted in South and Central America mostly in the 1950s. Buenos Aires is a rather low farm compared to other origins we buy, the farm spanning a range of 1200 - 1500 meters above sea level. I find their coffee to be very balanced, clean, mild to moderately bright and sweet, perhaps showing how relative farm altitude can be in relation to cup quality. This is our first of two lots from the 2020 harvest, but most of our Nicaraguas come from Cafetalera Buenos Aires mill in Ocotal and are of similar caliber.</p> </div>

<p>The Buenos Aires Caturra has a mild dry fragrance, a compound of raw sugar and vanilla, that when adding hot water shifts to smell of hazelnut-chocolate and dark caramel sweetness in the steam. Both our City+ and Full City roasts smelled and tasted quite clean for Nicaragua, a sugary sweetness balancing out the bittering roast tones. Buenos Aires is crowd pleasing in these middle roasts, a sweetness that spans raw cane-sugar to cooked brown sugar, with accent notes of vanilla bean and a chocolate wafer finishing note. Flavors are succinct, with a sweetly-disappearing aftertaste. It's a restrained cup, and balanced as you surpass the City roast level (though I do think City works for a brighter cup, just less sweet). The cup finishes with soft chocolate notes, a dried apple hint and black tea-like acidity is complimentary to the cup. As espresso, Buenos Aires is fantastic on it's own, but would also make a bittersweet base ingredient that will also add body to any blend.</p>
