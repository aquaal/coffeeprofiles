<div class="column-right">
<p>Elvis Rafael's coffee is grown in the El Diamante area of Jaen. Its close proximity to Jaen city has meant a large coffee cooperative presence and competition for coffee. Elvis's farm is 3 hectares, planted in Bourbon, Caturra and Catuai. El Diamante has a pretty wide range of altitude and Elvis' farm is beteween 1750 and 1900 meters above sea level. If you read the cupping notes, you can see that his coffee cupped with fruit-forward flavors - especially when put on a table of other Peruvian coffees. Part of this flavor profile is likely from his processing technic of pre-fermenting the cherry before wet processing. After the coffee is harvested, he lets the cherries sit for 12 hours which starts a sort of pre-fermentation. He then depulps the coffee and ferments for a full 24 hours before washing away the remaining fruit and drying for around 10 days. Elvis' coffee placed 7th and 25th at two different Cups of Excellence. You can certainly taste how it would stand out when put next to the more balanced-type Peruvian coffees</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>The coffee smells of brown sugar with hints of dried berry at City and City+ roast levels, somewhat more like a fruit jam underneath bittersweet roast smells at Full City. I sensed a wine-like ferment smell in the wet aroma that has a pleasing balsamic character and doesn't overpower the cooked sugar sweetness. The cup is bodied and balanced by turbinado-like sugary sweet flavors in both City and Full City roasts. Fruited top notes are impressive after the coffee has had time to cool and offer glimpses of dried apple, cantaloupe and a papaya aroma in the finish. Fruited flavors are well integrated with core sweet and bittering tones, especially in the lighter roasts. Full City roasting pulls out darker chocolate flavors that are challenged by a delicious blueberry note. </p> </div>
