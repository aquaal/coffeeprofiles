<div class="column-right">
<p>This small, 3 bag lot is from a single farmer in Perquin named José Inés. The town of Perquin is tucked away in the Cacahuatique mountain range. Finca La Victoria sits at 1750 meters, so it makes sense that it behaved like dense beans when roasting with relatively gentle first cracks without pulling back on heat at all. At 75 years old, Don José Inés grew up around coffee his whole life, but it was only 5 years ago that he started this farm. He belongs to a cooperative in Perquin, who with the help of a local NGO, have built out a central mill facility where coop members can wet mill their coffee and dry on communal patios. For most of the farmers it's a hike to get to the mill (7 hours!) but one of the better options for being paid the most for their coffee.</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>Nothing flashy here, but a well-balanced, rounded El Salvador coffee of the "daily drinker" variety from City+ to Full City+ roast level. The fragrance has a smell of toffee, malted chocolate and Nutella spread and the wet aroma lets off wafts of Peanuttle candies in the steam (toffee-coated roasted peanuts!). The cup is on the tame side, which is not a bad thing at all. The sweetness has a simple, light brown sugar flavor that offsets notes of roasted nut and bittering roast tones nicely. Full City roasts are chocolate-y bittersweet, flavor notes of chocolate torte, and baking cocoa in the long aftertaste. Espresso alert: La Victoria pushes creamy cocoa flavors to their limit at Full City. Will make a splendid cappuccino!</p> </div>
