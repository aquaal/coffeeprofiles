<div class="column-right">
<p>Rosemberg Martinez' 5 hectare farm, "La Ilusion" is located in the town of El Machín, not far from Tolima's capital city of Ibagué. His farm is planted almost entirely in Caturra, a natural mutation of Bourbon with a reputation for high cup quality when grown at high altitudes. At 1830 meters above sea level, the coffee from La Ilusion is certainly "high grown" and planted on the fertile slopes of the strato-volcano, Cerro Machín. Rosemberg practices traditional wet fermentation (depulping coffee, then fermenting in water-filled tanks as opposed to fermenting with no water), though for a full 48 hours, is on the long side. The long fermentation process no doubt plays a role in the fruited flavor characteristics we picked up on in the cup. We obtained this coffee via a buying project of an intermediary we work with who look for coffees and growing regions where high caliber coffees are produced, paying farmers a premium for the opportunity to separate out all of their coffees that meet a particular quality target. </p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>The flavors in the cup from Rosemberg Martinéz show signs of a coffee that underwent long fermentation time. The fruits that I pick up on have a slight 'boozy' quality, almost champagne-like in a way, typical of longer fermentation. The cup still has sweetness and acidity though, which lend structure and depth. Without those two characteristics, wine-y coffee can wind up tasting a bit like vinegar. Not the case here and this coffee presents sweet, albeit fruit-forward. City+ roasts have smells of apple cider, honey and sugar cane wine. The cider note transfers to the cup too and there's an aroma of slightly overripe apple. The sweetness is like panela sugar and a plum skin bittering quality plays into mouthfeel in the finish. Body is on the thin side and I'd recommend keeping this one to the lighter side of Full City max.</p> </div>
