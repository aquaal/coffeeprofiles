<div class="column-right">
<p>Remember our 2018 Ethiopia dry process "Hambela Hassan" coffee? This is from the same station owner, Kedir Hassan. "Dame Dabaye" is the name of the kebele where this Hambela station is located (a town, more or less). We were lucky enough to sleep at this station on our December 2018 visit sleeping in a pair of military tents replete with cots, overlooking the couple hundred drying tables below us. Kedir is a proficient and capable engineer as well as coffee farmer, and built the entire Hambela station in one season. You wouldn't know this, as it's visually apparent everything was built with high processing standards, and efficiency in mind. Of course he did not complete Hambela on his own, and thanks to his long standing connection with the the people in this area, he had much needed community support. Hambela sits at 1816 meters above sea level, and coffee is grown as high as 2100 meters. Check out the wet process lot we picked up from this same washing station <a href="https://www.sweetmarias.com/ethiopia-guji-hambela-dabaye-6609.html">HERE</a>.</p> </div>

<p>Light roasts have berry fruit and dried mango, a baking spice-laden fragrance, with some vanilla caramel impacting sweetness underneath. The aroma has a strawberry jam element and light floral hint, intense with fruit/berry smells, red fruits and tropical, not too rustic at all. City and City+ roasts are pretty amazing when brewed as pour over, berry notes perhaps the most evident right off the bat, fruit jams and some natural dehydrated types.  I preferred my lightest roasts of Hambela, where fruit and even a subtle floral aroma like orange blossom are present. The fruit flavors in the cup really come alive as the coffee cools off and showcase notes of boysenberry pancake syrup, dried blueberries and date. "Prune" is another flavor note I kept coming back to and feeds into a flavor combination that is like Dr. Pepper and tamarind flavored sodas. Full City roasts are also delicious but the fruited flavors are a little less clear and traded out for pleasantly bittering roast tones along with natural dried stone fruit and berry flavors. Hambella is certainly worth your consideration as an DP espresso as body is opaque and weighty and acidity easily tamed with deeper roast development.</p> </div>
