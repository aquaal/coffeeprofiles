<div class="column-right">
<p>"La Colibri" ("the hummingbird") is a project centered around small coffee producers in the area El Paraíso, Huehuetenango. The name signifies the small lots of coffee that some of the local farmers are producing, tiny "hummingbird" sized batches that on their own are a little difficult to manage. Farmer's Aurelio and Aler Villatoro saw an opportunity to organize a group of these famers to make harvest deliveries to their coffee mill on the same day. In this way, they would be able to process one larger batch of coffee from the farmer group, rather than several tiny harvest batches from each. The coffee is processed at Punta del Cerro mill, which sits at 1700 meters above sea level. The coffee farms span a range of about 1600 to 1800 meters above sea level and are mostly planted in Bourbon, Caturra, Catuaí and Typica coffee cultivars. The coffee is washed and fermented in water for about 24 hours, then dried on the cement drying patios of Punta del Cerro. They have indoor, greenhouse dryers as well, which at this time are reserved for natural process coffees.</p> </div>

<p>La Colibri has a uniquely fruited flavor profile for a wet process Guatemala that tastes a bit more like honey processing than fully washed to me. A smell of fruit pulp is released with grinding the roasted coffee, along with a fair amount of unrefined sugar type sweetness. The wet aroma shows more of a sugar caramelization sweetness, with fruit tones in the back end that carry with them winey aspects. In the brewed coffee, City and City+ roasts have fruited flavors and brightness of a young white wine. I realize that probably sounds ridiculous! But after the coffee cools down some, raw sugar and nut notes start to fall away, and the fruited hints underneath the bittersweet base flavors have characteristics of green grapes gone slightly over-ripe. I want to be clear, this coffee does not have the "boozy" qualities of a coffee that's been fermented too long. Rather, it is fruited but sweet, has acidity that adds structure to the cup, and finishes with complex baking spice aromatics. A complex and tasty light-roasted Guatemala.</p> </div>
