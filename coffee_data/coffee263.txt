<div class="column-right">
<p>Ata Gae is the area that sits at the peak of a ridge between Bajawa and Golewa near the Wawo Muda volcano on Flores island. The coffee is sourced from farmers at 1500+ meters above sea level, who are growing a mix of older Typica trees and S795, the latter commonly referred to as "Jember", a natural mutation of Typica and Liberica. The coffee is dried under plastic domes in order to mitigate the wet weather in the area and helps to facilitate even drying. Most of the coffees are processed on hand cranked or motorized depulping machines on the farm, fermentation in buckets or small tanks. Because of the nutrient rich soils, they are able to grow coffee using fully organic farm practices, though they are not certified at this time.</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>City+ roasts have clean sweetness in the dry grounds that along with bittering roast tones pull together a scent of dark chocolate muffin. The wet aroma has less of the chocolate roast tone and more of a prevalent brown sugar sweet smell with a mild green herbal note. The first cup characteristic that stands out to me is acidity as I'm used to Flores being on the mild side. No, this isn't "Kenya" bright, but Ata Gae has an acidic quality that is most like fruit juice at City+ and mouth-refreshing (and earned it a "light and bright" flavor tag - might be a first for Flores!). The cooling cup offers unrefined sugars and soft chocolate roast flavors with herbal top notes that come off like cilantro and celery soda. Full City roasts are still on the brighter side for Flores and lend structure to dense cocoa bittering. "Cocoa-dusted apple slice" came to mind when cupping. Both roasts are delicious pour-over brews and offer a brighter side to the "daily drinker" variety of coffee.</p> </div>
