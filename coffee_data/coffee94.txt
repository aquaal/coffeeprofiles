<div class="column-right">
<p>This was a top-scoring wet-processed coffee we selected late in this harvest season, sent to us by a private washing station owner in the region. His group do a very good job of managing the collection, separation, and milling, with intensive hand-sorting all along the way. Konga is the name of the town, or "Kebele", an area of Yirga Cheffe with a range of 1900-2100 meters. Smallholder farmers from around the region delivery their heirloom Ethiopian coffee varietals to the station where it is weighed, pulped and dried before being delivered to Addis Ababa. After drying, the coffee rests in its parchment layer for a few weeks before being run through hulling equipment and once again hand sorted as green coffee. Located very close to Yirga Cheffe town, the Konga area is a central hub for coffee in the region. This coffee was prepared to Grade 1 specifications when it was hand sorted in Addis Ababa before export. </p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>This Yirga Cheffe Konga coffee is both potent and refined. The dry fragrance shows an abundance of floral and stone fruit smells in light roasts with a strong jasmine note that tends to linger. Adding hot water brings up the florals a touch, stone fruit smells becoming much more apparent and a sweet scent of whipped honey in tow. The cup profile is undeniably articulate, and with mouth-cleansing acidity when roasted on the lighter end of the roast spectrum. A peach nectar flavor rings out in the cooling cup, along with apricot, pulpy orange juice, and mango. The base sweetness is complex, a honey undertone shifts toward baked sugar, layers unfolding as you move through the cup and a delicate floral note topping things off. The long aftertaste is accented by notes of ginger and a hint of rue herb in light roasts as well. Konga's complex cup flavors come to a nice point. Full City roasts are still loaded up with sweet fruits like blueberry and tropicaIs and a flavor of sweetened dark chocolate starts to build. I enjoyed my Full City just as much as City. But don't fly too close to the sun. Steer clear of going beyond Full City so as to retain it's a complex, fruited flavor profile.</p> </div>
