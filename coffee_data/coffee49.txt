<div class="column-right">
<p>Not to be confused with "Murambi" in Kiganda, another coffee we've bought in the past (and again this year too!), this lot is from Mutambu Commune near the Monge Nature Reserve in Bujumbura. The coffee washing station is at a lower elevation than the coffee, which grows in the surriounding the steep hillside reaching over 2000 meters above sea level. Murambi is the name of one of the hilltop villages, or "collines", and the farmers from Mutambu sell their coffee to the station below. They are one of 4 hilltop villages that the folks who run the wet mill buy from, the others being Rugembe, Masenga and Migoti. This is the first year we've bought coffee from this group, going all in with lots from each colline. The coffee is depulped using a 3-disc depulper, fermented with water and graded in long cement washing channels. Before drying on long, raised drying beds, the wet parchment drip-dries on small screens where any physical defects that can be identified by sight are removed. At 2000 meters, Mutambu Hill is the highest altitude group their buying from.</p> </div>

<p>The dry fragrance is laced with dark honey sweetness and accented by whole clove and a fruited note hinting at blackberry. The wet aroma smells lovely and oh so sweet. Dark sugar notes come through like sugar in the raw and a sachet of sweet baking spices infuses the steam. The hot cup starts off much simpler than the smells might lead on, but give it a little time to cool and the flavor profile starts to unfold. When hot, the underlying sweetness at City and City+ shows elements of raw sugar and honey that moves into bittering cocoa powder flavors in the aftertaste. Stick with it for a few minutes and the cooled coffee reveals hints of apple, clove, all spice, fragrant orange oil aroma and black tea. At Full City roast, a robust bittersweetness is interspersed with a blackberry note that adds a pectin-sweet, fruited highlight. Murambi makes a complex espresso shot at Full City - chocolate-y, with berry and spice notes - best to drink solo, without steamed milk.</p> </div>
