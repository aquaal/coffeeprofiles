<div class="column-right">
<p>Made up from coffees of several different farmers in the small town of San Francisco, within the greater District of Huabal, this 42 bag regional blend represents the coffees of many of the small holders in this region. Altitude range in "SF" spans a range from 1800 to 2000 meters above sea level and cultivars grown are typically Caturra, with some Typica and Bourbon mixed in. This is a fully washed coffee, wet processed by the farmers who then deliver their coffee in dry parchment (the green bean with the thin parchment layer still intact) to a warehouse in Cajamarca where it is graded and purchased. The group who set up this buying operation are working to register all the farmers they buy from in order to help isolate different quality tiers, but also be able to issue the initial payments and any quality premiums themselves, something that was lacking when they were buying through more traditional avenues. It doesn't help that the roads leading to Huabal and within the district are much more rustic than some of their neighbors, limiting access to farmers in this region.</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>Grinding the coffee of a City+ roast, a scent of dark chocolate and dried cherry is released. The bittersweetness found in middle roasts is balanced, and when brewed, presents a nice backdrop for subtle top notes to stand out against. The cup flavors start out with pungent toasted sugars and move into bittering cocoa notes like dark cacao bar and baking cocoa powder as you work your way through the cup. Red fruit come into view when the coffee cools off a bit and fruited notes hint at dried apple and golden raising when roasted light. San Francisco has a soft, fruited acidic impression too, especially at City and City+. Full City roasts boost the chocolate-y characteristics and you're left with flavors resembling German chocolate cake with a thin layer of berry filling. Do not be afraid to take into 2nd snaps if roast tones are what you're after. This Huabal coffee holds up to dark roasting, presenting you with an amalgam of deep chocolate roast flavors, mild berry hints and an all around full-bodied brew. An absolute must try single origin espresso too.</p> </div>
