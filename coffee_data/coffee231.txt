<div class="column-right">
<p>The Municipality of San Gaspar Ixchil is located near the Cuilco river, and not far from San Pedro Necta, another growing region where our coffee buying is focused. This particular lot is made up of a few neighbors in a village within San Gaspar, whose coffees shared similar cup characteristics (generally, somewhat fruited cups for Guatemala), and are complimentary to one another. "Xinabajul" is the name of the original Huehuetenango town, and also the name we've used for a project we've had going in the Huehue highlands for about 6 years now. "Proyecto Xinabajul" involves finding small holder farmers in the different growing regions who are producing great coffees, and then pay them a premium for allowing us to taste individual production batches in order to identify the best of the best. You can read more about the project <a href="https://legacy.sweetmarias.com/library/guatemala-proyecto-xinabajul/">HERE</a>. </p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>Mejor de Ixchil is a dynamic smelling coffee. The fragrance is potent, with unrefined sugar sweetness like panela and turbinado. Light fruited notes are found in the aroma at City roast and hints at raisin and fruit jam, while at Full City shifts to more of a fruited-cocoa slant. Brown sugar is the first flavor note I picked up on when cupping my City roast, along with a raisin hint, two familiar flavor notes for coffee from these parts and recalls oatmeal cookie dough. Mejor de Ixchil cools to sweet tones of marzipan, caramel popcorn, coconut chips, all blanketed in unrefined sugar sweetness that command more and more attention as the cup cools. The coffee has a really sweet finish that is marked by a complex cinnamon stick accent. Pear-like acidity is perceived at this roast level, both structuring and palate cleansing. Full City roasts retain a high powered bittersweetness, the cocoa notes at the first sip open up to to a fruited accent of dark grape. Mejor de Ixchil brews very well, and pour overs show a unique level of complexity for the region.</p> </div>
