<div class="column-right">
<p>This microlot was produced by Roger Solis at the highest part of his farm, "San Francisco 1900", the peak reaching just above 1900 meters above sea level. This particular lot is mechanically washed at Roger's micro-mill La Casona, which is surrounded by several different coffee plots similar to '1900' but at lower altitudes. They produce honey processed coffees too, but this is what is considered to be "fully washed" by Costa Rican standards. Instead of depulping and fermenting the coffee to remove the fruit, they use a machine called a mechanical demucilager that removes most of the fruit without scoring the seed. They then soak the coffee in clean water overnight, and lay to dry the following day. The resulting cup is a close approximation to "fully washed", usually clean in flavor, and capable of producing sparkling acidity. The city of San Marcos is in the Talamanca Sierra highlands, with peaks reaching as high as 3000 meters. It's an ideal micro-region and climate for growing coffee with rich volcanic soil, great altitude, and is shaded from the hot afternoon suns by mountain peaks.</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>This lot of San Francisco ("SF") 1900 has dark sugar in the cup, illuminating acidity, and nuanced finishing flavors that come to a point. Nut tones are limited in the dry fragrance to a scent of sugar-glazed almond, along with more prominent toffee sweetness. The wet grounds offer an all-around clean smelling aroma, toffee and butter caramels, along with a touch of dried fruit. City and City+ brews of San Francisco 1900 present rounded sweetness in the cup, with a tasty vanilla accent, and acidity that shines with the brisk tanginess of lemon flavored black tea. The cup is very sweet and shows flavors of unrefined sugars and dried fruit, fading to walnut and an ever so slightly bittering cocoa note in the finish. A fruited flavor came through in my middle roast, something like dried berry, and engulfed in bittersweetness. Chocolate roast tones are easily developed, and Full City roasts make for a nice dual-purpose coffee, producing a chocolatey brew as well as creamy, bittersweet espresso shot.</p> </div>
