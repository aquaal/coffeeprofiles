<div class="column-right">
<p>Not far from the shores of Lake Kivu lies Karambi, a washing station named after Karambi town where it resides. The altitude at Karambi station is 1700 meters above sea level and the coffee is grown upwards of 2000 meters in the surrounding mountainside. The station was erected to buy coffee from smallholder farmers in the region, many whom also work at the site processing and preparing the coffee for export. Quality grading starts immediately upon delivery, the underripe coffee separated out by floating in tanks of water. They produce wet-process ("fully washed"), honey process and dry process ("naturals") coffee at Karambi and all three undergo intensive sorting technics in order to make sure the A1 grade coffee is made up of the densest seeds. This is a fully natural, dry process lot with BIG dry process character that is as fruited as it is rustic. It's very versatile in the roaster, so have fun experimenting with it!</p> </div>

<div class="column-right">
<p>Karambi dry process spins a web of fruit and dark chocolate smells, strawberry and raw cacao come to mind and pouring the hot water brings up a sweet berry scent that reminds me of freeze-dried fruit. The cup shows intense dry process cup characteristics, rustic layers of palm and date sweeteners give way to fruited flavor notes like blueberry, tamarind paste and even a touch of grape. The rustic, earthy and fruit flavor tones gain intensity as the cup cools and it reminds me of the dry process Sumatra we recently had, a really wild and interesting coffee. The finish has a wheatberry note as well as a tannic mouthfeel that casts a slight acidic impression on the cup profile, though the acidity level scores a mild 8.0. I'm enjoying my City+ roast and would take this coffee as dark as Full City+. Just realize that at 2nd snaps you lose much of the fruit cup character and gain heftier chocolate roast flavors.</p> </div>
